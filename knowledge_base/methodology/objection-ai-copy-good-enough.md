# Objection: "AI-generated copy is good enough"

## The Objection
ChatGPT can write my copy in seconds. Why spend time researching customer language?

## Acknowledge
"For first drafts, brainstorming, and content at scale, AI saves a lot of time. For many use cases, AI-generated copy genuinely is good enough."

## Reframe
But "good enough" is not what makes copy convert. The copy that converts sounds like the reader's internal monologue: the specific phrases, metaphors, and emotional language that make someone stop and think "this person gets me."

AI tools produce polished, grammatically correct, **thoroughly average** copy. They don't know how your specific audience describes their frustrations on Reddit at 2 AM.

**XP Labs doesn't replace AI writing tools. It feeds them better input.** When you give ChatGPT real customer language as source material instead of a vague prompt, the output is noticeably better.

## Supporting Evidence
- AI defaults to generic phrasing because it draws from broad training data
- Verbatim customer phrases outperform AI alternatives in copy testing — authentic emotional weight
- XP Labs's excerpts export directly into AI writing prompts
- The best copywriters use AI as a tool but ground their work in real customer language

## How This Informs Campaigns
Frame XP Labs as the research step that makes AI tools AND human writers produce better output. Never position as anti-AI.

# Objection: "It's too expensive for what it does"

## The Objection
$29 to $499/month for Reddit research? I can do this for free.

## Acknowledge
"Pricing should make sense for what you get. And if you're doing occasional research, the free tier with 40 credits gives you a real test run — two full sessions before committing anything."

## Reframe
The question isn't what XP Labs costs. It's what your research time costs.

A manual session takes 2-4 hours. At the Interest tier ($29/month), you get ~5 sessions. That's 10-20 hours of manual work replaced for less than the price of a nice lunch.

And manual research is disposable. XP Labs research compounds — every session adds to a searchable library you draw from for months.

At Desire ($99/month, ~20 sessions), per-session cost drops below $5. Compare that to your hourly rate for the copy you write with that research.

## Supporting Evidence
- Interest: $29/month → ~5 sessions → replaces 10-20 hours manual work
- Desire: $99/month → ~20 sessions → <$5 per session
- Action: $499/month → ~110 sessions → heavy usage teams
- Credits valid 6 months — no "use it or lose it" pressure
- Fixed 20-credit pricing per session — no surprises
- Full refund on failed sessions
- 40 free credits on signup, no card required
- Top-up packs from 25 to 9,000 credits

## How This Informs Campaigns
Frame around ROI: time saved × hourly rate. For freelancers charging $150+/hour, $29/month replacing 10+ hours of work is trivial. Also emphasize compounding value — cost stays flat but value grows with usage.

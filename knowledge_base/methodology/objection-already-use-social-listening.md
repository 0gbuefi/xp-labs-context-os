# Objection: "I already use [social listening tool]"

## The Objection
Brandwatch / Mention / Sprout Social already monitors social conversations. Why do I need XP Labs?

## Acknowledge
"Social listening tools are great at tracking brand mentions, monitoring sentiment trends, and flagging conversation spikes. If you're using one for those purposes, keep using it."

## Reframe
Social listening answers "what are people saying about us?" XP Labs answers "what exact words should I use in my copy?"

Social listening gives dashboards, sentiment scores, and volume charts — built for brand monitoring and PR. They don't organize language by awareness stage. They don't extract verbatim excerpts tagged by research type. They don't generate voice profiles. And they don't help you write copy.

**XP Labs is not a social listening platform. It's a copywriter's research tool.**

## Supporting Evidence
- Social listening tracks mentions and sentiment; XP Labs extracts verbatim phrases
- Awareness-stage classification doesn't exist in Brandwatch, Mention, or Sprout Social
- Research tags (Pain, Dream, Obstacle, Trigger, Identity) map to copywriting frameworks, not brand monitoring
- Voice profiles synthesize research into a writing reference — no social listening tool does this

## How This Informs Campaigns
Most likely from [[persona-head-of-marketing]] who already has a social listening tool in their stack. Don't compete — differentiate the category entirely.

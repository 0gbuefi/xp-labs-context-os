# Persona: Freelance Copywriter

## Overview
Independent copywriter who handles client projects end-to-end. They do their own research, writing, and delivery. The buyer IS the user — they discover, evaluate, purchase, and use XP Labs themselves.

## Role Details

**Job title(s):**
- Freelance Copywriter
- Independent Copywriter
- Copywriter & Strategist
- Direct Response Copywriter

**Department:** N/A (solopreneur)

**Seniority level:** IC (independent contributor / business owner)

**Reports to:** Self (clients)

## Responsibilities

**Primary responsibilities:**
- Research target audience and voice-of-customer language
- Write sales pages, emails, ads, and landing pages
- Manage multiple client projects simultaneously

**Success metrics they're measured on:**
- Copy performance (conversion rates, reply rates)
- Client retention and referrals
- Speed of project delivery
- Quality of research backing their work

## Decision-Making Authority

**Buying power:**
- Budget control: Yes (100% — it's their business)
- Typical budget range: $29-$99/month for tools
- Approval authority: Immediate — no approvals needed

**Role in buying process:**
- Economic buyer AND end user (same person)

**Typical objections:**
- "I can just search Reddit myself" → [[can-search-reddit-myself]]
- "It's too expensive for what it does" → [[too-expensive]]
- "How is this different from ChatGPT?" → [[different-from-chatgpt]]

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - 2-4 hours per project on manual Reddit research
- [[pain-point-disposable-research]] - Research gets buried after each project
- [[pain-point-limited-query-angles]] - Only searching angles they think of

## Motivations & Goals

**Professional goals:**
- Write copy that converts (reputation depends on results)
- Build a reusable swipe file of customer language
- Deliver projects faster without sacrificing quality

**Personal motivations:**
- Charge higher rates by doing research-backed work
- Spend less time on mechanics, more time on creative writing

## Communication Preferences

**How they prefer to be reached:**
- Email (primary)
- Twitter/X DMs
- LinkedIn (less preferred)

**Messaging tone:**
- Peer-to-peer, informal
- Specific and practical (show, don't tell)
- Respect their craft — they know copywriting

**What they respond to:**
- Specific time savings ("2 hours → 90 seconds")
- Examples of real output (show excerpts, themes)
- Social proof from other copywriters

## Companies They Work At

- [[icp-freelance-copywriters]] - This IS the company
- [[icp-health-wellness-brands]] - Often writes for health brands
- [[icp-dtc-ecommerce-brands]] - Often writes for ecommerce brands

## Email Approach

**Subject lines that work:**
- Time-specific hooks ("2 hours of Reddit research?")
- Question-based ("Still copy-pasting Reddit quotes into Google Docs?")

**Opening hooks:**
- Reference the manual research process they already do
- Acknowledge their expertise — they already know research matters

**CTA that converts:**
- Free trial (40 credits, no card) — low friction
- "Try it on your next client project"

**What to avoid:**
- Talking down to them about copywriting basics
- Corporate/enterprise language
- Overexplaining what voice-of-customer research is

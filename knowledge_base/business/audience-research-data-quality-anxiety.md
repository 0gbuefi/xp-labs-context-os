# Audience: Research Data Quality Anxiety

This pain has two faces. First: "I can't tell what's useful and what's not" — copywriters include too much because they're afraid of missing something. Second (darker): "What if what I found is actually wrong?" — the fear that data is unreliable, forum posts dishonest, and an entire sales page built on false premises.

The nightmare scenario: you research, write, submit, the campaign bombs, and the client fires you. Daniel uses this in his sales narrative as a "knife twist."

## Audience Language

- "How do I know what is good to include in my research doc and what is not?"
- "I'm afraid I'll start trusting information that was actually false"
- "I never know if I actually found the real reason or the hidden desire of the market"
- "Am I finding all the surface level stuff that anyone else can find?"
- "Knowing exactly, ah, this is what I was looking for, would help massively with certainty"

## How This Informs Campaigns

XP Labs addresses this through volume and variety — when 30+ real Reddit threads surface the same pain in different words, you can trust it's real. Campaign angles:
- "How do you know your research is right? You don't. Unless 47 strangers on Reddit independently said the same thing."
- "XP Labs doesn't give you one data point. It gives you patterns across dozens of conversations."
- Position volume as validation: more sources = more confidence in data quality

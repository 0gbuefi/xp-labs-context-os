# Audience: The Research-to-Copy Translation Gap

This pain sits downstream of all other research pains. Even with good data, well-organized, copywriters stall at the blank page — they don't know how to bridge "I know what my market says" to "here's the opening line of the sales page."

Multiple respondents independently used the word "translating," implying research data is in one language and copy needs to be in another. Two layers: "What do I keep vs. throw away?" and "How do I structure the copy from this?"

## Audience Language

- "The translating part, the stage between research and writing the copy"
- "How do you translate all of that and turn the research into copy?"
- "What do you throw away? What do you keep? How do you know it's important?"
- "Not knowing how to extract those golden nuggets out of those huge research paragraphs"

## How This Informs Campaigns

XP Labs's awareness-stage tagging and theme organization partially bridges this gap — research arrives pre-organized for copy structure. Campaign angles:
- "Research organized by awareness stage means you already know what goes at the top of the page (problem-aware language) and what goes at the bottom (product-aware language)."
- Position XP Labs as making the translation step easier, even though it doesn't write the copy
- "The gap between research and copy isn't about having more data. It's about having the right data, organized the right way."

# Pain Point: No Access to Real Customer Language

Founders know their product but not how customers describe their problems. Marketers write copy based on internal assumptions, not the words prospects actually use. Survey data produces sanitized, performed responses. The result: copy that sounds like a marketer talking at customers, not a customer talking to a friend.

Reddit conversations are where people talk honestly — they vent, ask for help, and describe experiences without a filter. XP Labs taps into this unfiltered language.

## Key Points

- Founders describe their product using product language, not customer language
- Surveys produce sanitized responses (people tell you what they think you want to hear)
- Marketing copy based on assumptions sounds like a marketer, not the reader
- Anonymous platforms (Reddit) surface honest, unfiltered language
- The best copy sounds like the reader's internal monologue

## Evidence

[VERIFIED: xp-labs-context-os/knowledge_base/value-props/real-conversations.md] "People on Reddit do not perform for an audience. They vent, ask for help, share frustrations, and describe their experiences without a filter."

[VERIFIED: xp-labs-context-os/knowledge_base/value-props/messaging-matrix.md] "You know your product. You do not always know how your customers describe their problems."

## How This Informs Campaigns

Core pain point for founders and SaaS marketers. Lead with the gap between how they describe their product and how customers describe the problem:
- "You know your product inside and out. But do you know how your customers describe the problem it solves?"
- "Write marketing copy that sounds like your customers, not like a founder guessing."

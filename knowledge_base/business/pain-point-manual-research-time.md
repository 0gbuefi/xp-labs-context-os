# Pain Point: Manual Research Is a Time Sink

A thorough manual Reddit research session takes 2 to 4 hours: crafting queries one at a time, opening threads, scrolling through hundreds of comments, mentally sorting language by type, copy-pasting good phrases into a doc, and repeating. Most of that time is spent on mechanics — searching, reading, sorting — not on the creative thinking that actually makes copy better.

XP Labs replaces the mechanical work. 25+ queries, full thread analysis, theme extraction, and awareness-stage tagging in ~90 seconds.

## Key Points

- 2-4 hours per project is the typical manual research time for thorough work
- Most time goes to mechanics (searching, reading, sorting), not creative thinking
- Multiplied across clients/projects, this is a massive time tax on agencies
- XP Labs delivers the same depth in ~90 seconds

## Audience Language

Exact phrases copywriters use when describing this pain:

- "The time consumption"
- "Gathering all the data and organizing it"
- "It feels like a tremendous amount of work"
- "Research is boring"
- "Spending hours doing it and not actually finding anything of substance"
- "Having to do a lot of digging. I hate the time."
- "Sometimes I feel like I'm researching more than I'm writing"
- "I hate the time"
- "Confident I'm not wasting my time. If a client is waiting for you to finish a project and you spent two weeks doing the research when you actually had enough after two days, you've just wasted the client's time."

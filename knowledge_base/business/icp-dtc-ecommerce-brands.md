# ICP: DTC / E-commerce Brands

## Overview
Product brands selling direct-to-consumer that need authentic customer language for product descriptions, ads, email sequences, and landing pages. Reddit product discussions reveal unfiltered purchase triggers and objections.

## Company Profile

**Firmographics:**
- Industry/Vertical: DTC / E-commerce
- Company size: 5-100 employees
- Revenue range: $1M-$30M annually
- Geographic focus: US, Global
- Stage: Growth stage, scaling marketing spend

**Behavioral signals:**
- Running paid ads (Facebook, Google, TikTok)
- Writing product descriptions and email sequences
- Investing in conversion optimization
- Products discussed on Reddit (product subreddits, niche communities)

## Qualification Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Selling physical or digital products direct to consumer
- Writing their own marketing copy (or hiring copywriters)
- Products that people discuss in online communities

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Running A/B tests on product pages
- Investing in email marketing
- Active Reddit communities around their product category

**Red flags (disqualifiers):**
- Dropshipping businesses with no brand investment
- Pure marketplace sellers (Amazon-only, no own marketing)

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - Writing product copy based on features, not how customers describe value
- [[pain-point-generic-ai-copy]] - AI-generated descriptions that sound like every competitor
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Scouring product review threads and forums manually

## Personas

- [[persona-founder-marketing]] - Founder doing their own marketing
- [[persona-head-of-marketing]] - Marketing lead evaluating tools
- [[persona-content-marketer]] - In-house marketer writing copy

## Messaging Considerations

**What resonates:**
- Find the words shoppers use when they love (or hate) products like yours
- Purchase triggers and objections that drive buying decisions
- Exact customer language for product descriptions and ads
- Saturation analysis to know when you have enough research to launch

**What doesn't work:**
- Copywriter-specific jargon (awareness stages, swipe files)
- Complex methodology explanations

## Campaign Hooks

- "Your product descriptions describe features. Your customers describe outcomes. Find out what they actually say."
- "Reddit product threads reveal the exact words that make people buy (or bounce). Find them in 90 seconds."

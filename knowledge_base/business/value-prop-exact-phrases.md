# Value Prop: Exact Phrases, Not AI Summaries

XP Labs does not summarize what people said. It pulls out the exact phrases they used: verbatim quotes, emotional triggers, specific metaphors, and the precise words that make copy feel real. Themes tell you what to talk about. Excerpts give you the words to use.

## Key Points

- Verbatim quotes preserve the emotional texture that summaries lose
- Specific phrases outperform generic descriptions in copy testing
- Multiple excerpts on the same theme give variation for A/B testing
- Excerpts tagged by research type (Pain, Dream, Obstacle, Trigger, Identity, Objection)
- AI summaries flatten language — XP Labs keeps the raw, human language intact

## Evidence

[VERIFIED: xp-labs-context-os/knowledge_base/value-props/exact-phrases.md] "The difference between good copy and great copy is specificity. 'I'm frustrated with my job' is generic. 'I dread Sunday nights because I know Monday is coming' is copy you can use."

## How This Informs Campaigns

Core differentiator against both AI tools and social listening platforms. Use when audience understands the difference between AI-generated and human language:
- "XP Labs finds the phrases AI can't generate — the raw, specific, emotionally-textured words your customers actually use."
- "Themes tell you WHAT to talk about. Excerpts give you the EXACT WORDS to use."

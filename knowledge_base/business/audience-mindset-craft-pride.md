# Audience: Craft Pride and Love of Work

Craft pride follows a predictable emotional arc: newbie enthusiasm ("I just discovered copywriting and it's amazing!"), self-consciousness phase (downplaying the job), the flip (stops caring what people think), and open celebration. The community aspect is critical — copywriters find their tribe online with shared language and inside jokes that create strong in-group identity.

## Audience Language

- "the best job ever" (said with varying degrees of irony)
- "five years of insanely hard work and effort to master my craft"
- "I want to KEEP pinching myself"
- "one day ... I hope I can meet some copywriters who'll shake MY hand"
- "learning is endless. There isn't anyone who can claim to have mastered everything in this field."

## How This Informs Campaigns

Craft pride is the emotional fuel for marketing — copywriters who take pride in their work will invest in better tools. Campaign angles:
- "Good copywriters don't guess. They research." — appeals to craft pride
- Position XP Labs as a tool for serious craftspeople, not a shortcut for lazy writers
- "The research behind great copy is invisible. But you know it's there."
- In-group language: speak to copywriters like a fellow craftsperson, not like a vendor

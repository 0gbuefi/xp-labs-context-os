# Audience: Other Courses Didn't Solve It

RMBC (by Stefan Georgi) came up repeatedly in Daniel's survey. These copywriters took the course, followed its research module, and are still writing thousands of characters about their research frustrations. The pattern is broader than one course — copywriters invest in education and still feel stuck.

This means the audience are sophisticated, skeptical buyers who won't fall for surface promises. A tool (not just more education) might be what they actually need.

## Audience Language

- "I bought RMBC" / "I follow its research module"
- "Other copywriting courses I've done are really weak on the market research"
- "Not sure if that is because they don't want to give away their secrets or they just take it for granted"
- "It's not that systematic, it's mostly just me googling things"
- "I still feel like I don't have a system, and I feel bad about that"

## Evidence

[VERIFIED: Survey, Market Detective course survey, experienced copywriters] RMBC came up repeatedly as a course they'd taken but that didn't solve research problems.

[VERIFIED: Survey analysis narration by Daniel] Noted the pattern: even educated, experienced copywriters still describe the same research frustrations.

[VERIFIED: Survey, Market Detective course survey, experienced copywriter] Mention of Momoko's CXL course as another education investment that didn't close the gap.

## How This Informs Campaigns

This is critical positioning intelligence. Campaign angles:
- "You've taken the courses. You know the theory. You still hate research day. That's because you need a tool, not more theory."
- "RMBC taught you what to look for. XP Labs does the looking."
- Acknowledge their education: "You already know research matters. That's why you're frustrated — you know what good research looks like but can't get there efficiently."
- Don't oversimplify — these are experienced buyers who will see through hype
- Position XP Labs as the execution layer for knowledge they already have

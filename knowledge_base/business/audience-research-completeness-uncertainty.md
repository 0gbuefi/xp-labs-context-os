# Audience: Not Knowing When Research Is Enough

Copywriters don't stop researching because they feel done — they stop because they run out of time, energy, or patience. Unlike writing (where you have a finished draft), research has no obvious "you're done" signal. You can always find one more thread, one more review. Multiple respondents raised this independently across experience levels.

This is closely related to time burden but distinct: time burden is about how long it takes, this is about not knowing when it's acceptable to stop.

## Audience Language

- "I don't know when I have enough research"
- "How much research is enough?"
- "Knowing when to stop researching"
- "I'm not sure what my first step should be. I'm also not sure what should be my last step"
- "Am I going deep enough?"
- "I tend to worry that there's always more to learn about the market"

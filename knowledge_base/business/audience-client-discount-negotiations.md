# Audience: Client Discount Negotiations

How a copywriter handles discount requests reveals where they are in their career. Newbies cave. Experienced pros hold the line. And surprisingly, good clients actually WANT freelancers to push back — because willingness to walk away signals confidence in the value being offered. Non-neediness is the most attractive quality a freelancer can project.

## Audience Language

- "begging to be taken along, for whatever rate"
- "believe in their own value enough to tell me no"
- "what most freelancers never learn to do: the art of being non-needy"
- "a good client WANTS you to play hardball with them"

## How This Informs Campaigns

Discount negotiation connects to research confidence — better research = better copy = less price sensitivity. Campaign angles:
- "Hard to negotiate when your research is a Google Doc with 12 quotes. Easy when you have 47 analyzed conversations."
- "XP Labs doesn't just improve your copy. It gives you the evidence to justify your rates."
- The confidence → pricing → client quality chain: better tools → better work → better clients

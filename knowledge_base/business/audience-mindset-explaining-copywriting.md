# Audience: Explaining Copywriting to Outsiders

"So what do you do?" is the most dreaded question in the industry. The word "copywriter" means nothing to outsiders, creating a persistent identity friction. Copywriters love their work but live in a world where nobody understands it. The experience intensifies for those working from home who appear to not have a "real job."

Throssell lists 8 attempts he's tried to answer the question, from the earnest to the defeated.

## Audience Language

- "So what do you do?" followed by "blank stare"
- "It's too hard to explain what a copywriter does"
- "I basically get paid to write emails. And I can do it from wherever I want. It's kinda the best job ever LOL"
- "Daniel doesn't have a job, right?" (what outsiders assume)
- "copyright" vs. "copywriter" (the eternal confusion)

## How This Informs Campaigns

This is a strong community-building / relatability angle rather than a direct product pitch. Campaign angles:
- Email content that builds rapport by referencing shared identity pains
- "You know that blank stare when you tell someone you're a copywriter? At least your research doesn't have to be that confusing."
- Use as email opener / hook to build connection before pivoting to research pain
- Shows deep audience understanding — "this person gets what it's like to be a copywriter"

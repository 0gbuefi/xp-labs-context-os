# ICP: SaaS Companies (Marketing Teams)

## Overview
SaaS companies whose marketing teams A/B test headlines, write landing pages, run email sequences, and need authentic customer language to improve conversion rates across the funnel.

## Company Profile

**Firmographics:**
- Industry/Vertical: B2B or B2C SaaS
- Company size: 10-200 employees
- Revenue range: $1M-$50M ARR
- Geographic focus: US, Global
- Stage: Growth stage with established marketing function

**Behavioral signals:**
- Running A/B tests on landing pages and email subject lines
- Investing in conversion rate optimization
- Marketing team with copywriting responsibilities
- Using tools like Hotjar, Optimizely, or similar CRO tools
- Active in SaaS communities on Reddit, Twitter, and Slack

## Qualification Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Dedicated marketing team (not just founder doing marketing)
- Writing their own copy (landing pages, emails, ads)
- Doing some form of customer research or testing

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Already doing voice-of-customer research
- Running regular A/B tests
- Have a product with active Reddit/community discussions

**Red flags (disqualifiers):**
- Enterprise companies with rigid procurement (too slow)
- Pre-revenue startups with no marketing budget
- Companies that outsource all copy to agencies

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-generic-ai-copy]] - AI-generated landing page copy that sounds like everyone else's
- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - Marketing team guessing at customer language instead of using real words
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Research is a bottleneck that slows down campaign launches

## Personas

- [[persona-saas-marketer]] - Growth marketer or product marketer running campaigns
- [[persona-head-of-marketing]] - CMO or VP Marketing evaluating tools
- [[persona-content-marketer]] - Content team member writing blog posts and guides

## Messaging Considerations

**What resonates:**
- Stop A/B testing headlines you guessed — start with words your users already say
- Awareness-stage sorting maps to funnel stages
- Voice profiles for team-wide messaging consistency
- Compounding library as a shared marketing asset

**What doesn't work:**
- Copywriting-specific jargon (they think of themselves as marketers, not copywriters)
- Freelancer-focused messaging about swipe files

## Campaign Hooks

- "Your landing page copy was written by someone who's never read a Reddit thread about your product. Your prospects have."
- "Stop guessing what your customers say about [pain point]. Find out in 90 seconds."
- "Your competitors A/B test headlines they wrote. What if you started with the words your customers already use?"

# Persona: Content Marketer / Content Writer

## Overview
Produces blog posts, articles, guides, and thought leadership content. They need audience language to make content relatable rather than generic. Often found at SaaS companies, agencies, and media organizations.

## Role Details

**Job title(s):**
- Content Marketer
- Content Writer
- Content Strategist
- Senior Content Writer

**Department:** Marketing / Content

**Seniority level:** IC to Manager-level

**Reports to:** Head of Marketing or Content Director

## Responsibilities

**Primary responsibilities:**
- Research topics and audience language before writing
- Write blog posts, guides, email sequences, and landing pages
- Ensure content resonates with target audience
- Maintain consistent brand voice across content

**Success metrics they're measured on:**
- Content engagement (time on page, shares, comments)
- Organic traffic and SEO rankings
- Lead generation from content
- Content production velocity

## Decision-Making Authority

**Buying power:**
- Budget control: Influence only (recommends tools)
- Approval authority: Can trial free tools, needs approval for paid

**Role in buying process:**
- End user / Champion — discovers tool, advocates for purchase

**Typical objections:**
- "I can research topics myself" → [[can-search-reddit-myself]]
- "We already use SEO tools for topic research" → SEO tools find topics, XP Labs finds language

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - Content reads like a marketer wrote it, not like the audience talks
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Topic and language research is slow
- [[pain-point-limited-query-angles]] - Only finding the obvious angles

## Companies They Work At

- [[icp-saas-companies]] - In-house content team
- [[icp-content-marketing-agencies]] - Agency content writer
- [[icp-dtc-ecommerce-brands]] - Ecommerce content writer

## Email Approach

**Subject lines that work:**
- Content-specific ("Your blog sounds like a marketer. Your readers talk differently.")
- Topic discovery angle ("Reddit knows what your audience wants to read about")

**CTA that converts:**
- Free trial on next article project
- Show how theme extraction reveals content topics

**What to avoid:**
- Sales copy / direct response jargon
- Assuming they write ads or sales pages

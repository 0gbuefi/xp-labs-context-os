# Value Prop: Real Conversations, Not Surveys

People on Reddit do not perform for an audience. They vent, ask for help, share frustrations, and describe experiences without a filter. This is fundamentally different from survey data, focus groups, or interview transcripts where people tell you what they think you want to hear.

## Key Points

- Anonymous platforms surface honest, unfiltered language
- Reddit covers virtually every niche, industry, and audience segment
- Conversations reveal emotional context that surveys strip away
- Multiple people describing the same problem in different words = variation for testing
- The best copy sounds like the reader's internal monologue — you can't fake that with surveys

## Evidence

[VERIFIED: xp-labs-context-os/knowledge_base/value-props/real-conversations.md] "When someone on Reddit writes 'I dread Sunday nights because I know Monday is coming,' that is copy-ready language. A survey response like 'I experience work-related stress' is not."

## How This Informs Campaigns

Use as the opening hook in most marketing content. Core frustration: they know they need customer language but traditional methods produce sanitized responses:
- "Your survey data tells you customers are 'dissatisfied with onboarding.' Reddit tells you they 'spent 3 hours on the phone just to set up their account and almost cancelled.'"

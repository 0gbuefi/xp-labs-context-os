# Pain Point: Research Is Unorganized

Manually collected phrases end up in a messy doc with no structure. No themes, no awareness-stage tagging, no research-type classification. You have to sort everything yourself — figure out which quotes are about pain vs. desire vs. identity, which stage of the buying journey each one maps to, and which themes keep recurring.

XP Labs organizes automatically by theme, research type (Pain, Dream, Obstacle, Trigger, Identity, Objection), and awareness stage (Unaware through Most Aware).

## Key Points

- Manual research produces unstructured Google Docs of quotes
- No automatic theme grouping — you sort manually
- No awareness-stage classification — you decide where each quote maps
- Teams can't share or build on unstructured research docs
- XP Labs organizes everything automatically

## Audience Language

Exact phrases copywriters use when describing this pain:

- "A document of death of my own creation that has escaped my control"
- "It's about 100 pages and a thousand comments and doesn't make sense and is bloated and useless"
- "I kept adding categories in and kept adding in comments"
- "Was not really able to zero in on the key comments and have a document that made sense and was manageable"
- "Categorizing the stuff you come across"
- "Knowing where to put the desires, pain points, problems into your research doc to use later"
- "Sometimes it looks like a mess or you have to put things in multiple places because they tick more than one box"
- "Organization is annoying"
- "I want an easy way to organize and minimize my market research"

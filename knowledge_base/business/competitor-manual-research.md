# Competitor: Manual Reddit Research (Status Quo)

The most common "competitor" — people searching Reddit manually using Google with `site:reddit.com`, reading threads, and copy-pasting quotes into docs. It's free and requires no tools.

## Where It Falls Short

- **Limited query angles:** Only search angles you think of. Miss identity language, edge cases, contrarian views
- **Time-intensive:** 2-4 hours for thorough research
- **No organization:** Messy docs with no themes, no awareness stages, no tagging
- **Disposable:** Research gets buried, start from scratch months later
- **No quality scoring:** Read every thread equally, no prioritization

## XP Labs's Key Advantage

XP Labs doesn't replace the instinct to look at Reddit. It replaces the hours of mechanical searching, reading, sorting, and organizing, so you spend time on creative writing instead.

## How This Informs Campaigns

This is the primary competitor for most ICPs. The objection "I can just search Reddit myself" is the most common. Position against the time cost and depth limitations, not against the instinct to use Reddit:
- Acknowledge: "You're right, Reddit is great for this"
- Reframe: "The question isn't 'can I search Reddit?' It's 'how much time am I spending on searching instead of writing?'"

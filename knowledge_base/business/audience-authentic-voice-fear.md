# Audience: Authentic Voice Fear

This pain sits at the intersection of research and writing. Two dimensions: audience-facing ("I want to talk like my market like a guy who's known the market for years") and client-facing (clients rejecting work saying "that's good copy, but that's not how I would say this"). The word "pretentious" came up from multiple respondents independently.

Distinct from general imposter syndrome — this is specifically about whether research translates into authentic-sounding copy.

## Audience Language

- "I want to talk like my market like a guy who's known the market for years and shares the same frustrations as them"
- "Not sounding like a pretentious guy who's learned buzzwords about the industry"
- "You know the type of guy who doesn't understand something a lot but uses all kinds of industry jargon to fit in"
- "I'm tired of potential clients saying 'that's good copy but that's not how I would say this, we'll have to turn you down'"

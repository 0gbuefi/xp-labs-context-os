# ICP: Copywriting Agencies

## Overview
Agencies with teams of copywriters handling multiple client projects. They need consistent voice-of-customer research at scale and a shared library of customer language across the team.

## Company Profile

**Firmographics:**
- Industry/Vertical: Marketing / Copywriting Agency
- Company size: 3-50 employees
- Revenue range: $500K-$10M annually
- Geographic focus: US, UK, Australia (English-speaking markets)
- Stage: Established, actively serving clients

**Behavioral signals:**
- Multiple copywriters working on different client accounts
- Investing in research before writing (not just winging it)
- Charging premium rates for research-backed copy
- Using tools like Google Docs, Notion, or Airtable for swipe files

## Qualification Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Multiple active copywriting clients
- Copywriters on staff (not just strategists)
- Producing direct response or conversion-focused copy

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Already doing some form of Reddit/forum research manually
- Using AI writing tools alongside human copywriters
- Focused on specific niches (health, SaaS, ecommerce)

**Red flags (disqualifiers):**
- Pure SEO content mills (no voice-of-customer need)
- Agencies that only do brand/design (no copy)
- Very small agencies where founder does all writing alone (→ freelancer ICP instead)

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Each project requires hours of manual research, multiplied across clients
- [[pain-point-unorganized-research]] - Research scattered across Google Docs, no institutional memory
- [[pain-point-disposable-research]] - Past client research gets buried, team starts from scratch

## Personas

- [[persona-agency-copywriter]] - Does the research and writing, primary user
- [[persona-head-of-marketing]] - Agency owner/director who approves tools and budgets

## Messaging Considerations

**What resonates:**
- Team collaboration and shared research library
- Time savings across multiple client projects
- Compounding value — research builds over time
- Client/project organization keeping things navigable

**What doesn't work:**
- Messaging focused on individual use cases
- Pricing comparisons that ignore team value

## Campaign Hooks

- "Your team spends 2-4 hours per client on Reddit research. What if it took 90 seconds?"
- "Every research session your team runs adds to a searchable library. Six months in, you stop starting from scratch."
- "Shared workspace means when one copywriter finds gold, the whole team can use it."

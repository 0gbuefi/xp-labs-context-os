# Pain Point: AI Copy Is Generic

AI writing tools produce polished, grammatically correct, thoroughly average copy. They pull from broad training data and smooth out the emotional edges, quirky phrasing, and specific language that makes copy resonate. The output sounds reasonable but not real.

The difference: "I'm frustrated with my job" (AI-generated) vs. "I dread Sunday nights because I know Monday is coming" (real person on Reddit). XP Labs finds the second kind.

## Key Points

- AI flattens language — smooths out emotional texture
- Training data produces averaged-out, generic phrasing
- AI can't find what your specific audience says on Reddit right now
- XP Labs complements AI: feed real language into prompts for dramatically better output
- "Good enough" copy doesn't convert — specific, emotional copy does

Strong angle for the AI-forward ICP and for anyone using ChatGPT for copy. Position XP Labs as the research step that makes AI output better:

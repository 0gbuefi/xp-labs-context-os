# Persona: Founder (Marketing-Focused)

## Overview
Solo entrepreneur or small business founder who handles their own marketing copy. They know their product deeply but struggle to translate that knowledge into the language their customers actually use.

## Role Details

**Job title(s):**
- Founder
- CEO
- Co-Founder
- Owner

**Department:** Executive / Everything

**Seniority level:** C-Suite (founder)

**Reports to:** Self / Board

## Responsibilities

**Primary responsibilities:**
- Product development and business strategy
- Marketing and sales (wearing multiple hats)
- Writing landing pages, emails, and ad copy
- Customer development and research

**Success metrics they're measured on:**
- Revenue growth
- Customer acquisition cost
- Conversion rates on marketing
- Time efficiency across all roles

## Decision-Making Authority

**Buying power:**
- Budget control: Yes (100%)
- Approval authority: Immediate — sole decision maker

**Role in buying process:**
- Economic buyer AND end user

**Typical objections:**
- "I can just ask ChatGPT" → [[different-from-chatgpt]]
- "I know my customers already" → They know the product, not how customers describe problems
- "Too many tools already" → XP Labs replaces hours of manual work

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - Knows the product but not how customers describe their problems
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Time is the scarcest resource for a founder
- [[pain-point-generic-ai-copy]] - Using ChatGPT for copy that sounds like every other founder's marketing

## Companies They Work At

- [[icp-dtc-ecommerce-brands]] - Ecommerce founders
- [[icp-saas-companies]] - SaaS founders
- [[icp-health-wellness-brands]] - Health brand founders

## Email Approach

**Subject lines that work:**
- Challenge their assumption ("Your customers describe [problem] differently than you think")
- Time savings for busy founders

**CTA that converts:**
- Free trial — 40 credits, no card, under 2 minutes to launch
- "Try it on [their product category]"

**What to avoid:**
- Copywriting jargon (awareness stages, swipe files)
- Lengthy explanations — founders skim
- Assuming they have a marketing team

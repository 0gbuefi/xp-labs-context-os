# Audience: Running Out of Research Sources

The inverse of information overload. Some copywriters follow the standard advice (check Reddit, look at forums, read Amazon reviews) and come up empty. The market is too niche, the conversations don't exist, or everything they find feels fake and unusable. This is especially sharp for copywriters who work across different niches — some markets have rich online conversations, others are deserts.

Distinct from limited query angles (searching the wrong things) and data quality anxiety (finding things but not trusting them). This is about having nowhere left to look, period.

## Audience Language

- "Not finding enough spicy insights or any content at all"
- "The only thing I really hate is when I can't find much"
- "I'll run out of platforms"
- "What to do if I can't find enough"
- "You're running out of places to look and you have nothing"

## Evidence

[VERIFIED: Survey, Market Detective course survey, newbie copywriter] "I'd be miserable, beating myself up for hours doing vague research, not finding enough spicy insights or any content at all."

[VERIFIED: Survey, Market Detective course survey, experienced copywriter] "The only thing I really hate is when I can't find much. For example, I did a consultation for a virtual summit for parents who want to homeschool their kids and there's not a whole lot of market research to do on something so small and specific as that."

[VERIFIED: Survey, Market Detective course survey, newbie copywriter] "I'll run out of platforms."

[VERIFIED: Survey analysis narration by Daniel] "Everyone's told you to look at Reddit and forums and Amazon. So you look on Reddit. But nothing comes up. You check for forums but you can't find anything good. You start to get desperate. You check Amazon but there's nothing good except a bunch of rants and reviews that seem fake, although you have no way to tell. You're running out of places to look and you have nothing."

## How This Informs Campaigns

XP Labs's tiered pipeline approaches markets from multiple angles simultaneously — if direct searches come up dry, the AI generates focused and edge-case queries that find adjacent conversations. Campaign angles:
- Daniel's nightmare narrative is ready-made sales copy: "You check Reddit. Nothing. Forums. Nothing. Amazon. Fake reviews. You're out of options."
- "Some markets don't have a subreddit. XP Labs searches 25+ angles you wouldn't have tried."
- The homeschooling parents example is a perfect case study: niche market + limited sources = exactly where XP Labs's breadth matters most
- Position against the "just go to Reddit" advice: what happens when Reddit doesn't have your market?

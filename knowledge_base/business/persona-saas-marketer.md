# Persona: SaaS Marketer / Growth Marketer

## Overview
Growth-focused marketer at a SaaS company who A/B tests headlines, writes landing pages, optimizes funnels, and needs authentic customer language to improve conversion rates. Thinks in terms of experiments and data.

## Role Details

**Job title(s):**
- Growth Marketer
- Product Marketer
- Demand Gen Manager
- Marketing Manager

**Department:** Marketing / Growth

**Seniority level:** IC to Manager-level

**Reports to:** Head of Marketing / VP Marketing

## Responsibilities

**Primary responsibilities:**
- Write and A/B test landing pages, ads, and email sequences
- Optimize conversion rates across the marketing funnel
- Research customer language and positioning
- Run experiments to improve acquisition metrics

**Success metrics they're measured on:**
- Conversion rates (landing page, email, ad)
- A/B test win rate
- Pipeline contribution
- Customer acquisition cost

## Decision-Making Authority

**Buying power:**
- Budget control: Small tool budget ($29-$99/month is easy)
- Approval authority: Can expense tools under ~$100/month

**Role in buying process:**
- End user + potential economic buyer for individual license

**Typical objections:**
- "We use SparkToro for audience research" → [[vs-sparktoro]] — SparkToro shows where they go, XP Labs shows what they say
- "Our survey data is enough" → Surveys produce sanitized language, Reddit is unfiltered

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-generic-ai-copy]] - Landing pages that sound like every competitor's
- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - A/B testing headlines written by marketers, not customers
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Research bottlenecks campaign launch velocity

## Companies They Work At

- [[icp-saas-companies]] - Primary workplace

## Email Approach

**Subject lines that work:**
- Conversion-focused ("Your landing page copy was written by a marketer. Your prospects talk differently.")
- A/B testing angle ("Stop A/B testing headlines you guessed")

**CTA that converts:**
- Free session on their product niche
- Show awareness-stage mapping to funnel stages

**What to avoid:**
- Copywriting craft language (swipe files, voice profiles)
- Frame as marketing tool, not copywriting tool

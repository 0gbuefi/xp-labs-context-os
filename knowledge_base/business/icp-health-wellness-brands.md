# ICP: Health & Wellness Brands

## Overview
Supplement companies, fitness brands, and health/wellness businesses that need authentic emotional language — how real people describe symptoms, treatments, and frustrations — to write copy that resonates without sounding clinical.

## Company Profile

**Firmographics:**
- Industry/Vertical: Health, Wellness, Supplements, Fitness
- Company size: 5-100 employees
- Revenue range: $1M-$30M annually
- Geographic focus: US, Global
- Stage: Growth stage, investing in marketing

**Behavioral signals:**
- Selling supplements, fitness programs, health products
- Running paid ads on Facebook, Instagram, Google
- Producing email sequences, landing pages, product descriptions
- Navigating compliance constraints (health claims)

## Qualification Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Selling health/wellness products
- Writing marketing copy (ads, emails, landing pages)
- Need for authentic emotional language (not clinical terms)

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Active Reddit health communities around their product category
- Hiring copywriters or agencies for their copy
- Focused on specific health niches (supplements, gut health, fitness)

**Red flags (disqualifiers):**
- Pharmaceutical companies (regulatory complexity too high)
- Pure clinical/medical businesses

## Pain Points

- [[pain-point-no-customer-language]] - Customers describe symptoms emotionally, not clinically — copy needs to match
- [[pain-point-manual-research-time]] - Health forum research is time-intensive
- [[pain-point-generic-ai-copy]] - AI writes clinical-sounding copy that doesn't resonate emotionally

## Personas

- [[persona-founder-marketing]] - Health brand founder doing own marketing
- [[persona-head-of-marketing]] - Marketing lead at health/wellness company
- [[persona-freelance-copywriter]] - Health copywriter serving these brands

## Messaging Considerations

**What resonates:**
- Find how real people describe their health frustrations in their own words
- Reddit health communities are where people talk openly
- Research organized by client — supplement language never mixes with fitness language
- Emotional language behind clinical terms

**What doesn't work:**
- Generic SaaS marketing messaging
- Technical product feature dumps

## Campaign Hooks

- "Your customers don't say 'I have digestive discomfort.' They say 'I'm afraid to eat dinner because I know what happens at 2 AM.' Find the real language."
- "Reddit health communities reveal the emotional words behind clinical terms. That's where great health copy comes from."

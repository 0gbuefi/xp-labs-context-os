# Amazon Reviews VOC Mining

Amazon reviews are one of the highest-yield voice-of-customer research sources available — not just for products sold on Amazon, but for any market. The key insight: **you can always find books or competitor products in the same market**, and reviews on those contain the exact language your target audience uses to describe their problems, desires, and frustrations.

This technique works because Amazon reviewers are anonymous enough to be honest, reviews are plentiful, and the star-rating system lets you filter for specific emotional states (1-star = frustrations and unmet expectations; 5-star = dreams and desires fulfilled).

## Core Principles

- You don't need to sell on Amazon to use Amazon reviews — use adjacent products as proxies
- Books in the same market are always available and always have reviews
- 1-star and 2-star reviews reveal real pain, unmet expectations, and the specific language of frustration
- 5-star reviews reveal the dream outcome and what "success" looks and feels like to the buyer
- Reviews describe the journey: what they tried before, why it failed, what finally worked

## When to Use

Use Amazon review mining when:
- Starting research on any B2C-adjacent or consumer market
- Researching a niche where you can find competitor products
- Looking for books your target audience reads (virtually every niche has them)
- Needing high volume language data quickly
- Client is in a market where people buy and review physical products or books

**Works especially well for:**
- Health/supplements, fitness, finance, real estate, parenting, self-improvement
- Any market where people write about personal transformation or problem-solving

## How to Apply

**Step 1: Find proxies**
- Competitor products on Amazon in the same category
- Books targeting the same audience (search "[your market] guide", "[their problem] book")
- For B2B: management books, industry-specific titles, certification prep books

**Step 2: Filter reviews strategically**
- Read ALL 1-star and 2-star reviews → this is the frustration and pain language
- Read ALL 4-star and 5-star reviews → this is the desire and outcome language
- Look for reviews that tell a story, not just "good product" or "bad product"

**Step 3: Extract copy-ready language**
- Note specific phrases, metaphors, and comparisons
- Flag words that appear repeatedly — these are the market's natural vocabulary
- Capture the "before and after" structure in positive reviews
- Capture the unmet expectation structure in negative reviews

**Example (real estate services):**
Search "buying a house book" on Amazon → read reviews of popular titles → extract language about fear, excitement, confusion, what people wish they'd known.

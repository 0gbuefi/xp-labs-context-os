# Upvote as Resonance Signal

Reddit's upvote system does something invaluable for copywriters: it crowd-sources validation. When a post or comment receives many upvotes, hundreds or thousands of people have seen it and actively confirmed "yes, this is true for me too." That's not anecdote — it's consensus data.

This means you can trust upvoted content not just as one person's experience but as a reflection of what broadly resonates in the market. A highly upvoted post describing a pain point is a pain point you can write to with confidence.

The inverse is also true: as you scroll further down a thread and vote counts drop, reliability decreases. Rely less on low-vote content for copy decisions.

## Core Principles

- High upvotes = many people confirmed "this is me" — validated resonance, not anecdote
- Upvotes on posts surface the most resonant themes from the subreddit
- Upvotes on comments within a thread surface the most resonant descriptions of that theme
- Diminishing votes as you scroll = diminishing signal quality
- Upvotes are a built-in filter that replaces hours of manual pattern-finding

## When to Use

Apply this principle when:
- Deciding which Reddit posts to dig into vs. skip
- Prioritizing which comments to pull as quotes
- Validating whether a pain point is broadly felt or niche
- Deciding when to stop reading a thread (votes are getting very low)

## How to Apply

**Post-level filtering:**
- Sort by Top (not New) to surface highest-upvoted posts
- Posts with 100+ upvotes are safe to treat as representing a real, common experience
- Posts with 500+ upvotes represent core, high-resonance themes — definitely use these

**Comment-level filtering:**
- Within a thread, sort comments by Top or Best
- High-upvoted comments = the community agreed this is the most accurate or resonant description
- Look especially for upvoted comments that elaborate on the original post — these often contain the best copy language

**Threshold guidelines (rough):**
- 100+ upvotes on a comment: reliable signal, use with confidence
- 50-100 upvotes: good signal, worth noting
- Under 20 upvotes: lower confidence, look for corroboration elsewhere

**The "how do you know me" test:**
When a post or comment makes you think "how do you know me so well?" — and it has high upvotes — that's your signal you've found something copy-worthy. The reaction is the confirmation.

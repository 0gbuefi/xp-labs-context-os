# Facebook Ads Library Competitor Research

## What It Is

The Facebook Ads Library (`facebook.com/ads/library`) is a free public tool that lets you search any Facebook page and see every active advertisement they are currently running. It was created for political ad transparency but is usable for any advertiser.

For market research, it serves as a competitive intelligence window: **what angles, hooks, pain points, and offers are competitors using in their paid ads?**

---

## Why It's Useful for Research

Paid ads represent a company's most tested, highest-conviction messaging. If a competitor is running the same ad angle for weeks or months, it's almost certainly working. This gives you:

- **Validated pain language** — The copy in a long-running ad has been tested against the audience and proven to resonate
- **Proven angles** — What emotional hook or benefit claim is generating clicks (and presumably conversions)
- **Offer framing** — How they're packaging and presenting their front-end offer
- **What's not being said** — Gaps in competitor messaging that represent positioning opportunities

This is faster than mining Reddit for the same market — you're reading copy that's already been validated by spend.

---

## How to Use It

1. Go to `facebook.com/ads/library`
2. Select country (or "All" for widest view)
3. Search by the **Facebook page name** of a competitor or adjacent company
4. Browse all active ads — you can see images, video thumbnails, copy, and CTAs
5. Filter by date range to see only recent or long-running ads

**For research specifically:**
- Note the pain points and problem language in the copy
- Extract the benefit claims and outcome promises (these reflect the dream the market has)
- Identify the identity language (who they're positioning the product for)
- Record long-running ads (been live for weeks/months) as high-confidence validated language

---

## What to Do If Research Surfaces New Insights

When research uncovers something that wasn't in the product brief — for example, a pain point or objection the client didn't mention — **present it to the client as an interim report**. This positions you as a trusted advisor beyond an order-taker, and often opens conversations about product positioning, new offers, or overlooked market angles.

---

## Limitations

- Only shows **currently running** ads (ads that have been paused or deleted are not visible)
- Shows the ad creative but not performance metrics
- Some advertisers run limited or no Facebook ads — tool won't be useful for every competitor
- More relevant for B2C and B2B SMB; enterprise B2B companies often don't use Facebook ads heavily

# Research Saturation Stopping Criteria

One of the most common mistakes in market research is either stopping too early (not enough data) or never stopping (research paralysis). This framework gives concrete criteria for when online research is complete.

There are two types of stopping criteria: **natural saturation** (you've genuinely found enough) and **economic constraints** (the project doesn't justify more time). Both are legitimate reasons to stop.

## Core Principles

- The natural stopping signal is saturation: when new sources stop yielding genuinely new insights
- Economic reality matters: don't over-research a low-budget project
- Introverted research alone is sometimes sufficient — don't assume you always need to go further
- Gold in one subreddit can replace hours of other research — quality matters more than quantity of sources

## When to Use

Apply these stopping criteria:
- After any block of research time to assess whether to continue
- When deciding whether to move from introverted research to direct audience surveys
- When scoping a research phase for a new client project
- When a client asks "how much research are you doing?"

## How to Apply

### Signal 1: Natural Saturation

You've hit saturation when:
- You're reading new posts/reviews/comments but not finding new themes
- You can predict what the next comment will say before you read it
- The same phrases, stories, and frustrations keep recurring across sources
- Your notes are getting repetitive rather than growing

**Saturation is a feeling + a data check:** Stop feeling surprised by what you find, and verify your notes are indeed repeating.

**Critical: Personal immersion IS the detection mechanism.** The saturation feeling only emerges from reading the data yourself — "letting it wash over you, feeling the frequency of how often certain things come up." This is not just a way to detect saturation; it IS the instrument. Using AI to summarize or pre-process research before you read it destroys this signal — you receive conclusions without the experience that generates the feeling. See [[ai-limitations-for-market-research]].

### Signal 2: Found a Rich Source Early

Sometimes one subreddit or forum is so active and relevant that it provides everything:
- A subreddit dedicated specifically to the problem you're researching
- People are "spilling their guts" — sharing detailed personal experiences
- Upvotes confirm the most resonant content
- You can see months or years of ongoing conversation

In this case, you don't need to exhaust every other platform. You can stop earlier with high confidence.

### Signal 3: Project Constraints

Stop (or scope down) when:
- **No customer list to survey:** If the client can't provide a list for Stage 3 (market survey), introverted research becomes your primary data — accept its limits and move on
- **Project value under ~$1,000 / 10 hours:** The economics don't support deep multi-stage research. Do introverted research only and document what you found.
- **Client constraints:** Some clients won't allow access to their customer list or won't commission market surveys

### The Minimum Viable Research Standard

Even on low-budget projects, you should have:
- At least 2-3 sources explored
- Patterns emerging across sources (not just one data point)
- All four VOC buckets (identity, problems, dreams, obstacles) with at least some data
- Specific quotes you'd feel confident building copy around

If you don't have this, keep going. If you do, you can stop.

# Platform Prioritization

There's no single best platform for every market. Platform choice depends on where the audience actually congregates, how anonymous those platforms are, and what the niche looks like. A one-size-fits-all approach ("always use Reddit and Amazon") misses the mark for many markets.

This framework helps you quickly identify the right platforms for a specific research project.

## Core Principles

- No universal answer — the best platform is the one where your specific audience is active and honest
- Anonymity is the primary quality filter: more anonymous = more honest = better data
- Ask before assuming: the client often knows where their market hangs out
- Reddit and Amazon are good defaults but not universal — know when to go elsewhere
- The research will cross-link across platforms: Reddit threads reference websites, Google leads to forums

## When to Use

Use this framework at the start of every research project:
- When scoping research for a new client
- When briefing a researcher on where to look
- When the default platforms (Reddit, Amazon) seem thin for a particular niche
- When you need to justify your platform choices to a client

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Ask the Client

Your first best source for platform recommendations is the client themselves:
- "Where does your audience hang out online?"
- "What websites, communities, or resources do they use?"
- "What would they Google if they had your problem?"

Clients who are genuinely in their market usually know this. It saves you discovery time and often points directly to the richest sources.

### Step 2: Apply the Anonymity Filter

Rank candidate platforms by anonymity level and start with the most anonymous:

| Platform | Anonymity | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Reddit | High | Any niche with a subreddit; best default starting point |
| Niche forums | High | Specialized communities, trades, enthusiasts |
| Amazon reviews | Medium-High | B2C products, books in the market |
| YouTube comments | Medium | Visual/skills-based markets, how-to content |
| Blog comments | Medium | Industry-specific insight |
| News site comments | Medium | Markets with political/values alignment |
| Facebook groups | Low | Last resort; named profiles suppress honesty |

### Step 3: Match Platform to Niche

Some niches have obvious fits:
- **B2B SaaS, business topics:** Reddit (r/entrepreneur, r/sales, etc.), LinkedIn content comments, industry forums
- **Consumer products, health, supplements:** Amazon reviews, Reddit health subs
- **Crafts, hobbies, creative skills:** YouTube comments, niche forums, Reddit hobby subs
- **Real estate, finance:** Reddit personal finance communities, relevant books on Amazon
- **Trades, blue collar:** Niche forums (often still active), YouTube comments on how-to content

### Step 4: Verify with Google

When in doubt, Google the problem as the prospect would and see what surfaces organically. The top-ranking communities and content types for that search ARE the platforms your audience uses.

### Step 5: Let Platforms Cross-Reference Each Other

Don't silo your research. Reddit threads link to popular industry sites. Google searches surface forums. Industry blogs reference communities. Follow these leads — they're the market telling you where else to look.

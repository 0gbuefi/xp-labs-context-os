# Email Topic vs. Research Data

When mining Reddit or other platforms, every finding goes into the research document — but not every finding becomes a standalone email. Some data is background intelligence that makes you smarter about the audience; other data is specific enough to anchor an entire email.

Knowing the difference saves time and improves output quality. Research-only data builds context. Email-worthy data tells a specific story that a reader can see themselves in.

## Core Principles

- All research data belongs in the customer zooka document
- Only a subset of that data will make good standalone email topics
- Background data enriches sales pages, positioning, and general copy — don't discard it
- An email-worthy topic must have: a concrete scenario, emotional specificity, and enough material to develop a hook → body → CTA arc
- If you can't immediately see how to write an email from a finding, it's background data (for now)

## When to Use

Apply this distinction when:
- Working through research findings to identify campaign angles
- Deciding what to develop into email drafts vs. keep as reference
- Briefing a writer on what the research surfaced
- Prioritizing which threads/posts to explore more deeply

## How to Apply

### Signs That a Finding Is Email-Worthy

Ask these questions about each research finding:

1. **Is there a specific scenario?**
   - "I wake up and anxiety hits me in the face for the first 30 minutes" → YES (concrete moment)
   - "Anxiety is hard to explain" → MAYBE (interesting but vague — needs more)
   - "People with anxiety use Reddit" → NO (too meta)

2. **Does it have emotional specificity?**
   - "I don't want to get up, I don't want to talk, I just want to lie back down" → YES (vivid, specific)
   - "Anxiety affects my morning" → NO (generic)

3. **Is there a story arc?**
   - Problem: morning anxiety → Mechanism: cortisol → Solution: ways to lower cortisol → CTA → YES
   - Observation: people feel electric sensations → maybe, but no natural arc without more development

4. **Would the subject line write itself?**
   - "anxiety after waking" → immediately obvious, specific, relatable → YES
   - "difficulties communicating about anxiety symptoms" → vague, wouldn't perform

### Signs That a Finding Is Background Data (Not Email-Worthy Alone)

- Interesting but doesn't have a specific scenario to anchor a story
- Describes general audience characteristics, not a concrete moment
- Vocabulary or terminology observation (useful for writing tone, not a topic)
- Single data point without upvote validation

**These still go in the doc** — they inform tone, vocabulary, and positioning even if they never become a standalone email.

### The Development Test

A good email topic should feel immediately developable:
- You can see the subject line
- You can see the opening hook
- You can see the body (problem → mechanism or story)
- You can see where it connects to your offer

If you have to strain to see all four, it's probably background data.

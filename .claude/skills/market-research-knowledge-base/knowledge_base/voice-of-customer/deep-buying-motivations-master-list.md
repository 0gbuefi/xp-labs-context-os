# Deep Buying Motivations Master List

Every purchase decision is driven by one or more of these ~20 deep motivations. If you see them appear in research, the person is revealing a true motivation. If you *don't* see them, dig deeper — one of these is always underneath.

This is a reference list for recognizing real buying signals during market research and for writing copy that activates the right motivation.

## The Master List

### 1. Identity / Self-Concept (Most Powerful)
They want to become — or affirm — a certain type of person. Almost never stated directly. Almost always present.
- "I want to be the kind of person who..."
- Identity of skill (buy the chef's knife Gordon Ramsay uses → identity of professional chef)
- Identity of intelligence (WSJ subscription → "the informed, successful person")
- Identity of affiliation (team jerseys, political gear, brand loyalty)
- See: [[identity-as-primary-buying-driver]]

### 2. Embarrassment About a Problem
They have a problem they're ashamed of — bad breath, being overweight, not speaking their in-laws' language, not being able to defend their family.
- Signals: vague language, hedging, "I know it sounds silly but..."
- The embarrassment IS the motivator; don't minimize it

### 3. Guilt / Responsibility for Others' Suffering
They feel they're causing pain to someone they love.
- Dog barking waking up neighbors
- Snoring keeping spouse awake
- Chronic pain meaning you can't pick up your grandchildren
- This is distinct from personal pain — it's pain *because someone else suffers*

### 4. Fear of Being Exposed as a Fraud (Imposter Syndrome)
They fear others know things they don't, or will discover they're not qualified.
- Freelancers, consultants, creatives especially
- "What do the professionals know that I don't?"
- Buying courses = partly buying the identity of legitimacy

### 5. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
Fear of an opportunity closing, especially when others are taking it. A *push* motivator — amplifies other motivations rather than standing alone.
- Only works when there's an underlying desire; the scarcity accelerates the decision

### 6. Sense of Duty
A deep-seated obligation — to family, country, community, ideals.
- Signal language: "it's just the right thing to do", "I owe it to..."
- "I want the identity of being the breadwinner / provider / protector"
- People have died for duty — it is serious motivation

### 7. Giving Children Better Opportunities
As a parent, wanting your children to have what you didn't have.
- Distinct from personal achievement — it's about legacy and children's futures
- Activates both duty (I owe this to them) and identity (good parent)

### 8. Freedom From Constraints
Trapped by money, time, or responsibilities — and want out.
- Financial: can't afford the things they want
- Time: job with no schedule control
- Responsibility: caregiving, kids, family obligations consuming life

### 9. The "Unlimited Spending" Domain
Everyone has at least one area where they'll spend anything. The giveaway phrase: "only the best" or "it's worth it."
- Could be a niche hobby, a pet, skiing, a collection
- In this domain, price is not an objection — it's irrelevant

### 10. Feeling Deserving After Hardship
They've earned the right to something through suffering, effort, or sacrifice.
- "I've worked so hard this year, I deserve this holiday"
- "I've put up with this slow phone long enough"
- Mums who've sacrificed for their kids want to spend on themselves

### 11. Loneliness / Wanting Love and Connection
The desire for physical or emotional connection. Manifests differently by gender.
- Women: emotional connection, being understood, belonging
- Men: often physical or social ("I want to be the kind of person others gravitate toward")

### 12. Fear of Death / Mortality
Awareness of their own finite life and fear of dying or aging.
- Health products, life insurance, legacy-building
- Particularly powerful when triggered by a family member's death or diagnosis

### 13. Fear of Losing Independence
Financial: running out of money in retirement. Physical/mental: losing capability due to health decline.
- "My parents had Alzheimer's — I know what's coming"
- Deeply personal and rarely stated explicitly; look for euphemisms around "staying sharp," "staying active"

### 14. Insecurity About Self-Defense / Protection
Feeling unable to protect themselves or people they love.
- Physical weakness (man who can't defend his family)
- Personal safety (woman who fears for her safety)
- Property (homeowner afraid of home invasion)

### 15. Wanting to Look and Feel Good (For Self)
Not for others — for their own mirror moment.
- Men: stronger, more muscular, more lean
- Women: feeling beautiful, reversing aging
- Distinct from "attracting others" — this is internal validation

### 16. Wanting to Attract Others / Social Status
Looking good, impressive, or admirable to other people.
- Men: admired for strength, looks, or wealth
- Women: more attractive than other women
- Universal: being envied, impressing in-group
- External status markers: flashy car, latest phone, designer clothes

### 17. Stopping Ongoing Pain or Discomfort
Chronic, nagging pain — physical or mental — that wears them down.
- Physical: back pain, chronic illness, hay fever
- Mental: stress, anxiety, persistent fear
- Sleep disruption: baby waking, snoring, barking dog
- Key signal: they've been living with it long enough that they're *done*

### 18. Sexual Pleasure
Self-explanatory. Present in many more markets than you'd expect.

### 19. Wanting Wealth / More Abundance
Basic desire for more money, more luxuries, more experiences (travel, etc.).
- "Get rich quick" succeeds because the desire is genuine
- Often combined with freedom motivation

### 20. Impressing Others and Earning Respect
Wanting to be admired — for a strong golf swing, a charming personality, a flashy car.
- Reputation management in professional contexts
- Social proof as motivation (I want what the respected people use)

### 21. Revenge / Proving People Wrong
Wanting to show up people who've doubted, dismissed, or wronged them.
- A boss who didn't believe in them
- A disapproving parent
- A controlling ex-partner ("you'll never survive without me")
- Any group perceived as taking from them

### 22. Fear of Losing What They Have
Protecting existing wealth, property, relationships, or status from threats.
- Insurance, security products, legal protection
- Distinct from "wanting more" — this is a loss-aversion motivation

### 23. Wanting Results Faster
Time impatience. "Lose weight NOW." "Get fluent in 3 months."
- Almost universally applicable — you can almost always improve an offer by promising the result faster
- Not the deepest motivation but one of the most universal

### 24. Wanting Things Easier
Effort aversion. "Lose weight without dieting." "Learn a language without studying."
- Pairs with speed motivation — people want fast AND easy
- Like speed, almost universally applicable as a copy amplifier

## How to Use This in Research

When reading Reddit posts, reviews, or forum threads:

1. **If you see these signals clearly stated** → you've hit a real motivation, document it
2. **If you see hints** → dig deeper in the research, look for more specific language
3. **If someone says something generic** (e.g., "my health is important to me") → it's probably one of these underneath (usually identity or appearance)
4. **If nothing matches** → keep reading; one of these is always there

The most important: **identity** (#1) is almost always present and almost never stated. Look for it beneath every other motivation.

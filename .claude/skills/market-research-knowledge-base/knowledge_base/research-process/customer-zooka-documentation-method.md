# Customer Zooka Documentation Method

The "customer zooka document" (also called the market research doc) is the running document where all research findings are recorded during introverted research. It's the central output of the research phase — the place where raw observations become organized, usable copy intelligence.

The key insight is that this document is **flexible and customizable**. It starts with standard categories (identity, problems, dreams, obstacles) but expands to include topic-specific subcategories whenever the market warrants it. For example, a physical condition like anxiety might get a dedicated "what does it feel like?" section that a business-to-business market wouldn't need.

## Core Principles

- There is no one-size-fits-all template — adapt the document structure to the market
- Build it incrementally: note things as you find them, not after the session
- Include direct quotes with enough context to understand them
- Organize by theme/category, not chronologically
- The document tells you when you're done: when you keep adding to the same categories without new ones emerging, you've hit saturation

## When to Use

This document is created and maintained:
- At the start of every new client research project
- Whenever doing introverted research on a new market
- Throughout the research phase (keep it open and add to it as you go)
- As a living document that accumulates across multiple research sessions

## How to Apply

### Default Document Structure

Start with the four VOC buckets as sections:

```
## Identity
[Notes on how they describe themselves, their role, their worldview]

## Problems
[Direct quotes and paraphrases of how they describe their pain]

## Dreams
[Language around outcomes, what they want life/work to look like]

## Obstacles
[What they've tried, why it failed, what's in their way]

## Notes / Miscellaneous
[Observations that don't fit cleanly, platform patterns, vocab notes]
```

### Customizing for the Market

Add subcategories when you notice a recurring theme that deserves its own section:

- **Physical/health markets:** Add "What does it feel like?" section for sensory/experiential language
- **Technical markets:** Add "How they describe their level of expertise" section
- **B2B markets:** Add "How they talk about their role/responsibilities"
- **Any market:** Add "Common vocabulary / insider terms they use"

### What to Record

For each finding:
1. A direct quote (copy-paste, not paraphrase)
2. Source URL or at minimum platform + context
3. Upvote count if from Reddit (for signal strength reference)
4. Brief note on which category it belongs to and why

### Note-Taking in Real Time

Don't batch your notes. Keep the document open while researching and add as you go. The act of deciding "which category does this belong to?" sharpens your understanding of the market as you work.

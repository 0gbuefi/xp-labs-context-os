# 30-Day Pilot Framework

A proven cold offer pattern for B2B lead generation agencies: Promise 5 qualified calls in 30 days for $1,500 (half upfront, half at completion). This specific structure has driven "crushing" results across multiple lead gen agencies by solving cold traffic's core uncertainty.

The framework works because it's finite, specific, produces new money, and doesn't overextend operational capacity.

## Core Principles

**Offer structure:**
- **Outcome:** 5 qualified calls (specific, countable)
- **Timeframe:** 30 days (finite, feels controllable)
- **Price:** $1,500 total ($750 upfront, $750 at end)
- **Positioning:** Pilot/proof phase before backend retainer

**Why this structure:**
- **5 calls** = Achievable without overextending (not 10, not "as many as possible")
- **30 days** = Short enough to feel low-risk, long enough to deliver
- **$1,500** = Meaningful revenue, but accessible for SMBs
- **50/50 payment** = Commitment from client, but backend payment creates accountability

**Critical:** This is a FRONT-END offer. Backend sells retainer ($3k/month) or pay-per-call after pilot proves results.

**Payment psychology:**
- "$750 upfront + $750 at completion" feels smaller than "$1,500 upfront"
- Backend payment = you have to deliver (creates trust)
- Split structure reduces perceived risk
- Can also frame as "$300 per call" to show value per outcome

## When to Use

Use 30-day pilot when:
- Selling lead gen services to cold traffic
- Prospects object: "I don't have case studies with you"
- Market is skeptical of lead gen effectiveness
- Competing with agencies offering vague "pipeline optimization"
- Client close rates are reasonable (20%+ so you can deliver 5 calls)

**Don't use when:**
- Client can't close (need 20%+ close rate minimum)
- Market has 6+ month sales cycles (won't see results in pilot window)
- You can't operationally deliver 5 calls in 30 days consistently

## How to Apply

### Implementation Steps:

**1. Set operational baseline**
Calculate if 5 calls in 30 days is achievable:
- Can you safely assume 20-30 calls booked per 90-day period?
- If yes, 5 calls in 30 days = conservative
- If no, you're not operationally ready (fix fulfillment first)

**2. Structure the offer in email**
```
Subject: 5 calls in 30 days?

[Personalization]

We'll get you 5 qualified calls with [ICP] in the next 30 days.

$1,500 total. $750 upfront, $750 when we deliver.

[Social proof if available]

Want details?
```

**Key elements:**
- State price directly (creates clarity vs competition)
- Mention timeframe upfront (finite = safe)
- Simple structure (stranger can say yes immediately)
- Include price breakdown to show payment structure

**3. Backend transition strategy**
After delivering 5 calls in 30 days:

**Option A - Retainer:**
"We delivered 5 calls. Ready to scale to 20/month on retainer? $3k/month."

**Option B - Pay per call:**
"We delivered 5 calls. Continue at $X per qualified call?"

**Option C - Creative retention (Quarterly bonus):**
"Close one of these calls in 45 days, we'll discount the pilot cost over 12 months"
- $1,500 ÷ 12 months = $125/month discount
- Pilot feels "free" psychologically
- Locks in 12-month commitment
- Everyone motivated to close deals quickly

**Why Option C works:** People get excited about "winning back" the pilot cost, creating urgency to close deals and commit long-term.

**4. Manage stipulations**
If offering guarantee ("or you don't pay"):
- Require 25% close rate minimum
- Must respond to leads within 24 hours
- Must have clear qualification criteria
- Be transparent about requirements upfront

**Math example:**
- 90 days = reasonably expect 20-30 calls booked
- 30 days = 5-7 calls achievable
- At 25% close rate: 20 calls = 5 clients
- Stipulation is reasonable and achievable

**Don't bait-and-switch.** Requirements must be reasonable and disclosed.

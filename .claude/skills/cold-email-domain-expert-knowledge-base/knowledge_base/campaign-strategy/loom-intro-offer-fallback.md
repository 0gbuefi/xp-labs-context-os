# Loom Intro Offer Fallback

When you can't find a winning offer, offer to send prospects a **custom Loom video** about your solution. Gets instant market feedback, nearly 100% yes rate, and can book first clients.

## Core Principles

- **Last resort when nothing else works** - not first choice
- **People always say yes** to receiving custom Loom
- **Market feedback machine** - see where they drop off
- **Zero risk ask** - just watching a video
- **Learn what resonates** - apply learnings to campaigns
- **Can book clients** - Mitchell booked first 6 clients this way
- **Systemizable parts of service** - offer valuable snippet from onboarding

## When to Use

Use Loom intro offer when:
- Can't find any offer that works (tested 12+ variants)
- Zero traction from traditional approaches
- Need market feedback desperately
- Starting agency with no clients
- Want to understand what messaging resonates
- High-value prospects worth custom outreach

## How to Apply

### Simple Loom Offer

**Pattern:**
```
Subject: saw something interesting

Hey [first name],

Saw [specific thing about their business/content/market].

Can I send you a quick Loom about [relevant insight/opportunity]?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- No commitment required (just watching video)
- Curiosity trigger ("what did they see?")
- Custom = feels personal, not mass outreach
- "Quick" = low time investment

**Example:**
```
Subject: noticed something

Hey Sarah,

Saw you're posting founder-led content consistently. Noticed
your engagement is strong but curious if it's turning into
pipeline.

Can I send you a 3-minute Loom showing how to convert those
engaged followers into calls?

- Mitch
```

### Loom Content Strategy

**What to include in the Loom:**

**Structure (3-5 minutes max):**

1. **Hook (15 sec):**
   "Hey [name], thanks for saying yes to the Loom. I noticed
   [specific observation about their business]."

2. **Diagnosis (60 sec):**
   "Here's what I'm seeing and why it matters..."
   Point out specific problem/opportunity

3. **Solution mechanism (90 sec):**
   "Here's how you could solve this..."
   Explain your unique approach (high-level)

4. **Social proof (30 sec):**
   "We've done this for [similar companies]..."
   Quick case study or result

5. **CTA (15 sec):**
   "If you want to discuss how we could do this for you,
   reply to this email or book here: [link]"

**Analytics you'll learn:**
- Where they drop off → what's not resonating
- If they watch full video → strong interest signal
- If they rewatch sections → what matters most to them
- If they share link → viral indicator

### Intro Offer Variant (Non-Loom)

**If you have systemizable service components:**

Offer valuable piece from your main service:

**Examples:**

**Onboarding snippet:**
```
"We have a 5-step onboarding process for new clients. The
first step is where we identify your winning message.

Want me to do just that first step for you (free)? You'll
get a 1-page brief of your best angles to test."
```

**Sales process value:**
```
"The first call we do with every client is a messaging audit.

Want me to record that audit as a Loom for you? You'll see
exactly what we'd do different."
```

**Form with skin in the game:**
```
"I can send you a creative/audit/analysis, but I'll need you
to fill out this 5-minute form first so it's actually
relevant to your situation."
```

Why form works: Qualification + time investment = serious prospects only.

### Mitchell's First 6 Clients Strategy

**Context:** Starting agency, no clients, no credibility

**Approach:**
```
Subject: Loom?

Hey [first name],

Saw you're [specific observation].

Can I send you a Loom about an outbound strategy that could
work for [their market]?

- Mitch
```

**Process:**
1. 3,000 people messaged
2. High yes rate (Loom ask is easy yes)
3. Sent custom Loom for each
4. Watched analytics (where they dropped off)
5. Refined messaging based on patterns
6. Booked first 6 clients in 30 days

**Learning:** Loom offered free market research + booking opportunity.

### When NOT to Use

**Don't default to Loom offer when:**
- Haven't tested 12+ offer variants yet
- Can get traction with simpler offers
- Loom doesn't scale (manual work per prospect)
- Market is huge (can't do custom videos for thousands)

**Loom is fallback, not primary strategy.**

Mitchell: "If you can't, for the life of you, figure out any offer that is working, literally anything, please do an intro offer."

### Scaling Loom Strategy

**If Loom offer works:**

1. **Template the script** - same structure, customize names/details
2. **Batch record** - record 5-10 at once
3. **Identify patterns** - what points resonate across all viewers
4. **Extract into campaign** - turn best-performing Loom points into email copy
5. **Transition to scalable offer** - use Loom learnings to craft offer that works without custom videos

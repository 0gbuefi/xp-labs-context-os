# Offer Testing: 30-40 Word Statements

Test offers as **30-40 word simple statements** that ask "if I did X, would you be interested?" No guarantees, no free trials, no complexity - just clean signal on what resonates.

## Core Principles

- **Simplicity enables testing** - complex offers obscure what's actually working
- **No guarantees during testing** - introduces confounding variable
- **No "do it for free"** - can't tell if offer or price point is issue
- **Just ask interest** - "Would that be interesting?" / "Would that be helpful?"
- **Pain points vs outcomes** - first place to test offers
- **One thing per test** - isolate what you're learning
- **8th grade reading level** - adjust complexity down after drafting

## When to Use

Use 30-40 word offer testing when:
- Starting campaign ideation with new client
- Market is unclear what they care about
- Previous complex offers failed
- Need clean data on resonance
- Testing multiple angles quickly
- Have 12+ test variants ready to launch

## How to Apply

### Formula Structure

**Pattern:**
```
If I could help you [OUTCOME/SOLUTION] for [TARGET MARKET/CONTEXT]
[TIMEFRAME], so you could [BENEFIT/USE CASE].
Would that be interesting?
```

**Example 1: Message-market fit discovery**
```
If I could help you identify the exact message that resonates
to [target market], I would definitely do in the next 45 days.
So you could use it on your ads and founder-led content to make
sure it resonates to who you want buying from you.

Would that be interesting?
```

**Example 2: LinkedIn engagement to pipeline**
```
I know you're doing content already. You have hundreds of people
engaging with you every day.

If I could help you send relevant messages on LinkedIn and email
to all the people in your market engaging with you, to send them
a lead magnet or intro offer, to get more calls of the content
you're already posting.

Would that be helpful?
```

### Pain Points vs Outcomes Testing

**Focus areas:**
- 💰 **Gaining money** - revenue increase, cost savings, ROI
- ⏰ **Winning back time** - efficiency, automation, delegation

**Frame without numbers initially:**

❌ Don't start with: "I will save you 70%" or "Increase 40%"
✅ Do start with: "Help you reduce X problem" or "Enable you to achieve Y"

**Why:** Percentage claims mean nothing without unique mechanism. Test the mechanism first, add data once mechanism resonates.

### Process

**Step 1: Draft the offer**
Write 2-3 sentences describing the transformation.

**Step 2: Simplify to 30-40 words**
Cut everything non-essential. Get to core value prop.

**Step 3: Check reading level**
Adjust to 8th grade reading level or below. If it sounds fancy, simplify.

**Step 4: Add see-feel-touch words**
Incorporate sensory language that helps visualization (see [[see-feel-touch-words]]).

**Step 5: Consistent CTA**
Keep call-to-action identical across tests:
- "Would that be interesting?"
- "Would that be helpful?"
- "Would that be worth a discussion?"

**Don't test CTA and offer simultaneously** - isolate offer testing first.

### Testing Protocol

**Volume per test:**
- Minimum 100 sends per offer variant
- Ideally 200-300 sends for statistical confidence

**Timeline:**
- 12 tests on day one (if infrastructure supports 1,000+ sends/day)
- Find winners within 48 hours
- Enables rapid iteration cycles

**What to test:**
1. **Different pain points** - which problem matters most?
2. **Different outcomes** - which result is compelling?
3. **Different mechanisms** - which "how" resonates?
4. **Different timeframes** - 30 days vs 45 days vs 90 days?

**Don't test:**
- Guarantees (introduce later after winner found)
- Pricing (test offer resonance first)
- Social proof (add after mechanism validated)
- Complex case studies (noise during testing)

### After Finding Winner

Once you identify winning 30-40 word offer (8%+ positive reply rate):

**Then introduce:**
- Social proof variants
- Case study examples
- Humor / personality
- CTA variations
- Intro line personalization

**Frame changes to test:**
- Industry-specific language
- Role-specific framing
- Company stage variations

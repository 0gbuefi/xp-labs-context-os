# Worldview Alignment Targeting

Target prospects based on **their worldview** - what they're actively investing in, what data signals about their beliefs, how they see the world. Creates "category of one" positioning where you feel like a trusted expert who understands their situation better than they do.

## Core Principles

- **Worldview = framing of your offer** - not what you do, but how you position it
- **Data signals worldview** - what they invest in reveals beliefs
- **Context creates relevance** - old vs young founder have different worldviews
- **One-of-one perspective** - your unique take on their industry/market
- **Trusted expert positioning** - understand their situation better than them
- **Active investment = worldview indicator** - what they're already doing matters
- **50% close rates possible** - when worldview alignment is strong

## When to Use

Use worldview alignment targeting when:
- Standard demographic targeting isn't converting
- Need higher close rates (30-50%+)
- Have narrow ICP with specific characteristics
- Can identify investment patterns via data
- Building category-of-one positioning
- Want prospects entering calls "ready to buy"

## How to Apply

### Identify Worldview Signals

**Investment Patterns:**
- What they're actively spending money on
- What strategies they're executing
- What tools/platforms they use
- What content they create

**Examples:**

**Signal:** Founder doing founder-led content
**Worldview:** Believes in personal brand building
**Offer frame:** "Help you turn content engagement into qualified pipeline"

**Signal:** Old founder (65+) with large team
**Worldview:** Delegation, established processes, team leverage
**Offer frame:** "Systemize outbound so your team can scale without you"

**Signal:** Young founder (25-35) with lean team
**Worldview:** Scrappy, hands-on, efficiency-focused
**Offer frame:** "Do more with less - automate what doesn't need you"

**Signal:** Solo founder (no co-founder)
**Worldview:** Self-reliant, control-oriented, resource-constrained
**Offer frame:** "Turn your limited bandwidth into predictable pipeline"

**Signal:** Founder with co-founder
**Worldview:** Collaborative, shared decision-making
**Offer frame:** "Align your outbound with your partnership strategy"

### Frame Outcomes Around Worldview

**Standard offer (no worldview):**
```
"We help B2B SaaS companies get more leads."
```
Generic. Could be anyone.

**Worldview-aligned offer:**
```
"I help founders doing founder-led content turn their LinkedIn
engagement into qualified pipeline - so the audience you're
already building actually books calls."
```
Specific. Only relevant if they do founder-led content.

### Targeting Criteria Evolution

**Traditional targeting:**
- Title: VP Sales, CMO, Founder
- Company size: 50-200 employees
- Industry: B2B SaaS
- Revenue: $5M-$50M

**Worldview targeting:**
- Title: Founder
- **+ Signal:** Posts LinkedIn content weekly (founder-led content)
- **+ Signal:** Engagement >100 likes/post (has audience)
- **+ Signal:** Team <20 people (still hands-on)
- **Frame:** "Turn your engaged audience into pipeline"

**Result:** 50% close rate because worldview alignment creates "ready to buy" positioning.

### Building Category of One

**What this means:**
You're not "another lead gen agency" or "another cold email provider."

You're the **only person** who helps [specific worldview] achieve [specific outcome] in [specific way].

**Example structure:**
```
"I'm the only person who helps [founders doing founder-led content]
achieve [qualified pipeline from existing engagement] through
[AI-powered outreach to their warm audience]."
```

**Why it works:**
- Narrow targeting = less competition
- Worldview match = instant relevance
- Unique mechanism = differentiation
- Trusted expert = "This person gets me"

### One-of-One Perspective

**Your unique take on their market/industry:**

Not: "Cold email is broken"
But: "Founders building audiences waste 80% of their engagement - here's why"

Not: "You need better leads"
But: "Your content engagement is pipeline sitting in plain sight"

**Mitchell's hint:**
"I'll give you a hint here because I don't want to give away my client's offer because that specific targeting is helping my client close at a 50% close rate because the alignment is so high that they enter the call ready to buy."

This means:
1. He identified a worldview signal (what they actively invest in)
2. He framed the offer around that worldview
3. Prospects see it and think "This is exactly for me"
4. They enter calls assuming they'll buy, not evaluating

### Scraping Worldview Data

**What to look for:**

**LinkedIn profile:**
- Do they post content? (Frequency = commitment level)
- What topics? (Worldview on market/industry)
- Engagement levels? (Audience size/quality)

**Company website:**
- What lead magnets do they offer? (Worldview on value delivery)
- What case studies? (Who they see as ideal customer)
- What messaging? (How they position themselves)

**Job postings:**
- Hiring SDRs? (Worldview: scale through team)
- Hiring content creators? (Worldview: inbound marketing)
- Hiring engineers? (Worldview: product-led growth)

**Use data to personalize:**
```
"Saw you posted 12 blogs this month - clearly committed to
content strategy. What if you could turn those readers into
qualified calls?"
```

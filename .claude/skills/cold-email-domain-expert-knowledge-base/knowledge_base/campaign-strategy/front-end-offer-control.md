# Front-End Offer Control

The core problem in B2B lead generation: client offers don't work on cold traffic. The solution: agencies stop accepting client offers and instead CONTROL the frontend offer presented to cold prospects. This shifts agencies from "traffic delivery" to "offer + traffic" - solving both variables instead of one.

**Strategic insight:** "By end of 2026 most people will do, I'm 90% certain is they will look to control the front end offer."

## Core Principles

**The Lead Gen Problem:**
1. Client has offer (usually warm offer requiring trust)
2. Agency runs cold traffic to that offer
3. Offer doesn't convert cold traffic
4. Agency blamed for poor results
5. Agency can't control the core variable (the offer)

**Two additional problems agencies can't control:**
1. The offer (what's being sold)
2. Sales conversion (client's close rate)

**Result:** Agency stuck between bad offer and bad sales = impossible to deliver

**The Solution:**
Control problem #1 (the offer) by designing the frontend offer yourself

**Benefits:**
- Widens TAM (total addressable market)
- Works across more client types
- Proven offer pattern reduces risk
- Agency becomes more valuable (offer design + traffic)
- Better results = better retention

## When to Use

Use front-end offer control when:
- Client has valuable service but weak cold offer
- Client's backend is strong but frontend is "optimization," "consultancy," "management"
- You want to scale lead gen without being limited by client's offer quality
- Working with industries where offers are typically weak (consultancy, agencies, SaaS)

**Especially critical for:**
- Growth consultancies (can't sell consultancy cold)
- Marketing agencies (can't sell "management" cold)
- SaaS companies (can't sell "platform" cold without trial/POC)
- High-ticket B2B services (need trust-builder first)

**Ideal client criteria with controlled frontend:**
- Has backend service worth selling
- Has budget (inferred from team size, revenue, etc.)
- Can close deals reasonably well (20%+ close rate)
- Backend offer is valuable/scalable
- Open to frontend offer design

**You don't need:**
- Their existing offer to be "good"
- Case studies in their exact niche
- Them to understand cold email

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Build Frontend Offer Library

Create proven cold offer patterns for different markets:

**Example library:**

**For B2B SaaS:**
- Frontend: 30-day free pilot with 3 power users
- Backend: Annual subscription

**For Marketing Agencies:**
- Frontend: Single campaign strategy using their best case study (free)
- Backend: Full campaign management retainer

**For Consultancies:**
- Frontend: Complete GTM strategy + mockup landing page (free)
- Backend: Implementation partnership

**For Lead Gen Clients:**
- Frontend: 30-day pilot (5 calls, $1,500)
- Backend: Retainer or pay-per-call

**Pattern recognition:**
All frontends are: specific, finite, low-risk, provable value

### Step 2: Qualify Based on Backend + Capacity

**New qualification criteria:**

Instead of: "Do they have a good offer for cold?"
Ask: "Do they have a valuable backend + can they close?"

**Checklist:**
- ✅ Backend service is valuable/scalable?
- ✅ They can close at 20%+ rate?
- ✅ ICP is clear enough to target?
- ✅ Budget exists (team size, revenue indicators)?

**You control the frontend offer design, so their "offer" quality doesn't matter**

### Step 3: Present Frontend Offer as Part of Service

**Positioning shift:**

**Old pitch:** "We'll get you calls with your ICP"
- Assumes their offer works
- You're just traffic source

**New pitch:** "We'll run this frontend offer [proven pattern] to your ICP, then convert them to your [backend service]"
- You control both offer + traffic
- You're strategic partner, not vendor

**Example script:**
"You're selling [backend service]. That's valuable but hard to sell cold. We'll run a [frontend offer] that gets cold prospects to engage. Once they see value, we convert them to your full service. We've used this pattern across [X clients]."

### Step 4: Design Custom Frontend for Client

**Process:**

**Step 1: Understand their backend**
- What do they actually sell long-term?
- What's the real value they deliver?
- What results do clients get?

**Step 2: Identify frontend that builds to backend**
- What quick win/proof project leads naturally to backend?
- What can be delivered in 30-60 days that proves capability?
- What mechanism is logical and novel for their market?

**Step 3: Apply cold offer principles**
Test against ultimate offer question:
- Can stranger say yes without trust? ✅
- Specific and finite? ✅
- Produces new money/opportunity? ✅

**Step 4: Map transition**
- How does frontend success lead to backend conversion?
- What's the pitch after frontend delivery?
- What incentives drive transition?

### Step 5: Control Two Variables, Not Just Traffic

**Old model:**
Agency → Traffic → Client's Offer → Client's Sales → Results
(Control: Traffic only)

**New model:**
Agency → Frontend Offer Design + Traffic → Backend Offer → Client's Sales → Results
(Control: Offer + Traffic)

**Even better model:**
Agency → Frontend Offer + Traffic + Sales Support → Backend → Results
(Control: Offer + Traffic + conversion support)

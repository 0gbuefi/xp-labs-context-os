# See-Feel-Touch Words (Sensory Language)

Use **see-feel-touch words** (sensory language) to help prospects **visualize** what you're saying. Transforms abstract offers into concrete, tangible concepts that prospects can mentally experience.

## Core Principles

- **Sensory = memorable** - people remember what they can visualize
- **Abstract → Concrete** - turn vague concepts into specific images
- **Three senses:** See, Feel, Touch (visual, emotional, tactile)
- **Worldview alignment amplifier** - sensory words + worldview = powerful resonance
- **Not flowery language** - still conversational, just more vivid
- **Positions against competition** - "putrid LinkedIn inmails" vs your approach

## When to Use

Use see-feel-touch words when:
- Offer is abstract (needs concrete framing)
- Differentiating from competition
- Describing pain points (make them visceral)
- Painting vision of outcome
- Want prospect to "feel" the transformation
- Standard language isn't resonating

## How to Apply

### See Words (Visual)

**Help prospects visualize the concept:**

**Abstract:** "We'll help you find the right message"
**With see-words:** "We'll help you **uncover** the hidden message that resonates"

- Uncover (visual: removing a cover to reveal)
- Expose
- Illuminate
- Spotlight
- Crystal-clear
- Foggy (negative)
- Murky (negative)

### Feel Words (Emotional/Tactile)

**Help prospects experience the emotion or sensation:**

**Abstract:** "Bad LinkedIn messages"
**With feel-words:** "Those **putrid** messages you receive from InMail requests"

- Putrid (visceral disgust)
- Smooth
- Rough
- Crushing (weight/pressure)
- Suffocating
- Liberating
- Draining

### Touch Words (Tactile)

**Help prospects sense physical interaction:**

**Abstract:** "Marketing challenges"
**With touch-words:** "The **grinding** daily battle with lead quality"

- Grinding
- Friction
- Slipping through fingers
- Grasping
- Gripping
- Sticky
- Slippery

### Examples from Mitchell

**LinkedIn differentiation:**
```
"Not like those putrid messages you receive from inmail
requests on LinkedIn."
```

**Why it works:**
- "Putrid" = visceral, sensory (smell/disgust)
- Positions his approach as opposite
- Memorable (vs "bad messages")

**Farming metaphor (for agricultural ICP):**
```
"It's almost like year after year of cloudy weather and no rain.
What's that going to do to your crops?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Visual: cloudy weather (see)
- Consequence: crops failing (see)
- Emotional: anxiety about livelihood (feel)
- Relevant to farmer worldview

**Uncovering (discovery frame):**
```
"Help them uncover the hidden insights in their data"
```

**Why it works:**
- "Uncover" = visual (removing cover)
- "Hidden" = implies treasure beneath surface
- More compelling than "find insights"

### Pattern: Negative Positioning

**Use sensory words to describe the bad old way:**

**Bad:** "Unlike other agencies..."
**Good:** "Not like those **putrid** InMail requests..."

**Bad:** "Better than competitors..."
**Good:** "No more **drowning** in unqualified leads..."

**Bad:** "More effective approach..."
**Good:** "Stop **spinning your wheels** on cold calling..."

### Pattern: Outcome Visualization

**Use sensory words to paint the future state:**

**Abstract:** "You'll have more qualified leads"
**Sensory:** "Picture your calendar **filled** with qualified calls"

**Abstract:** "Better email deliverability"
**Sensory:** "Your emails **landing** in primary inbox, not **buried** in spam"

**Abstract:** "Improved close rate"
**Sensory:** "Prospects entering calls **ready to buy**, not just exploring"

### Worldview + Sensory Combination

**Most powerful:** Align sensory language with prospect's worldview

**For founder-led content creator:**
```
"Your engaged audience is pipeline sitting in plain sight -
let's pull those warm leads out of the comments and into
your calendar."
```

- "Sitting in plain sight" = visual (hiding in open)
- "Pull out of comments" = tactile (extraction action)
- "Into your calendar" = visual (filling calendar)

**For technical founder:**
```
"Your data silos are suffocating your team's productivity.
Let's crack them open and let information flow."
```

- "Suffocating" = feel (can't breathe)
- "Crack them open" = tactile + visual (breaking barrier)
- "Flow" = visual (liquid movement)

### Application Process

**Step 1: Write offer in plain language**
```
"We help B2B companies get more meetings from cold email"
```

**Step 2: Identify abstract words**
- "help" (vague)
- "get" (vague)
- "more meetings" (abstract quantity)

**Step 3: Replace with sensory equivalents**
```
"We help B2B companies fill their calendars with qualified
meetings that actually show up and convert"
```

- "Fill their calendars" = visual
- "Show up" = physical action
- "Convert" = transformation

**Step 4: Add worldview alignment**
```
"Saw you're drowning in LinkedIn engagement but not booking
calls. We help founder-led content creators turn those warm
comments into qualified meetings filling your calendar."
```

- "Drowning" = feel/visual (overwhelm)
- "Turn" = transformation action
- "Filling your calendar" = visual (abundance)

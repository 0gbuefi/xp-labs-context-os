# Email Length: 80 Words Maximum

Elite cold email performers (top 10%) average **under 80 words** per first-touch email. Brevity forces clarity - every word must earn its place. This benchmark represents the sweet spot between providing enough context and respecting prospect time.

## Core Principles

- **80 words = maximum** for first-touch emails
- **Elite performers consistently** hit this benchmark
- **Brevity forces clarity** - no room for fluff
- **Every word must earn its place** - ruthless editing required
- **Enough to communicate value** - not too short, not too long
- **Respects prospect time** - can read in 30 seconds
- **Higher performance** than longer emails empirically proven

## When to Use

Use <80 word limit when:
- Writing first-touch cold emails (Email 1 in sequence)
- Targeting busy executives and decision-makers
- Want to maximize reply rates
- Testing new campaigns (start tight, expand if needed)
- Emulating elite performer patterns
- Industry benchmark recommends it

**May exceed 80 words when:**
- Follow-up emails (Step 2+) adding case study details
- Warm audiences who already know you
- Complex offers requiring context (still aim for brevity)
- Educational content where prospects requested info

## How to Apply

### The 80-Word Structure

**Standard structure fitting within 80 words:**

```
[Personalized opener - 1 sentence, 10-15 words]
[Problem/challenge statement - 1-2 sentences, 15-25 words]
[Your solution/offer - 1-2 sentences, 20-30 words]
[Simple CTA - 1 sentence, 10-15 words]

Total: 55-85 words (target: 70-80)
```

**Example (78 words):**
```
Hey Sarah,

Saw you're scaling your SDR team from 5 to 15 reps this quarter.

Most teams struggle when they double headcount - ramp time
stretches from 60 to 90 days and quota attainment drops below 50%.

We built a playbook system that keeps ramp at 45 days even during
hypergrowth. Used by 12 B2B SaaS companies scaling through Series B.

Would 15 minutes next week make sense to walk through how it works?

Best,
Mike
```

**Word count: 78**

### How to Hit 80 Words

**Start by writing freely, then edit ruthlessly:**

**Draft 1 (142 words - too long):**
```
Hi Sarah,

I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out because I
noticed on LinkedIn that your company is in the process of scaling
your SDR team from 5 representatives to 15 representatives this
quarter, which is really exciting.

When companies go through this kind of rapid growth phase, they
often encounter challenges that can be difficult to navigate.
Specifically, most teams experience significant struggles when they
double their headcount - the ramp time for new hires tends to
stretch from an ideal 60 days all the way out to 90 days or more,
and quota attainment rates frequently drop below the 50% threshold,
which can be concerning for leadership.

Our company has developed a comprehensive playbook system...
```

**After ruthless editing (78 words - perfect):**
```
Hey Sarah,

Saw you're scaling your SDR team from 5 to 15 reps this quarter.

Most teams struggle when they double headcount - ramp time stretches
from 60 to 90 days and quota attainment drops below 50%.

We built a playbook system that keeps ramp at 45 days even during
hypergrowth. Used by 12 B2B SaaS companies scaling through Series B.

Would 15 minutes next week make sense to walk through how it works?
```

**What was cut:**
- ❌ "I hope this email finds you well" (generic filler)
- ❌ "I wanted to reach out because" (unnecessary preamble)
- ❌ "on LinkedIn" (irrelevant detail)
- ❌ "which is really exciting" (opinion, not value)
- ❌ "this kind of rapid growth phase" (verbose)
- ❌ "can be difficult to navigate" (vague)
- ❌ "Specifically" (filler word)
- ❌ "or more" (unnecessary hedging)
- ❌ "which can be concerning for leadership" (obvious)
- ❌ "comprehensive" (adjective adds no value)

**What was kept:**
- ✅ Specific numbers (5 to 15, 60 to 90 days, 50%, 45 days)
- ✅ Clear problem statement
- ✅ Concrete solution
- ✅ Social proof (12 companies)
- ✅ Simple, clear CTA

### Editing Checklist

**Pass 1: Remove filler phrases**
- [ ] "I hope this email finds you well"
- [ ] "I wanted to reach out"
- [ ] "Just wanted to check in"
- [ ] "I noticed that"
- [ ] "It would be great if"
- [ ] "I was wondering if"

**Pass 2: Cut unnecessary adjectives**
- [ ] "comprehensive"
- [ ] "innovative"
- [ ] "cutting-edge"
- [ ] "revolutionary"
- [ ] "game-changing"

**Pass 3: Replace phrases with words**
- "in order to" → "to"
- "due to the fact that" → "because"
- "at this point in time" → "now"
- "for the purpose of" → "to"
- "in the event that" → "if"

**Pass 4: Combine sentences**
- Look for adjacent sentences that can merge
- Use em-dashes or commas to connect related ideas
- Eliminate repetitive phrases

**Pass 5: Count and trim**
- Run word count
- If >80, identify lowest-value sentence
- Cut or compress until <80

### Word Count by Component

**Target allocation:**

| Component | Words | Percentage |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Personalized opener | 10-15 | ~20% |
| Problem statement | 15-25 | ~30% |
| Solution/offer | 20-30 | ~35% |
| CTA | 10-15 | ~15% |
| **Total** | **55-85** | **100%** |

**Flexibility:**
- Adjust ratios based on complexity
- Problem-focused emails: 40% problem, 25% solution
- Solution-focused emails: 25% problem, 40% solution
- Always keep CTA under 15 words

### Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Explanation overload**
```
Bad (156 words): "Our platform uses machine learning algorithms
combined with natural language processing to analyze historical
email patterns and..."

Good (18 words): "Our system analyzes your email history and
suggests replies. It's like having an assistant draft responses."
```

**Mistake 2: Credential dumping**
```
Bad (94 words): "Our company has been in business since 2015 and
has raised $40M from top-tier VCs including Sequoia and a16z. We
serve over 500 enterprise customers across 40 industries and have
won numerous awards including..."

Good (22 words): "Used by 500+ enterprise customers including
Salesforce, HubSpot, and Gong. Series B funded, processing 10M
emails daily."
```

**Mistake 3: Soft openings**
```
Bad (32 words): "I hope this email finds you well. I know you're
busy so I'll keep this brief. I wanted to reach out because..."

Good (7 words): "Saw you're hiring 10 SDRs this quarter."
```

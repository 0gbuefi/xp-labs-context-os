# XP Labs Copywriting Standards

**RIGID GUIDELINES - NON-NEGOTIABLE**

These are the hard rules every XP Labs cold email campaign must follow. No exceptions.

---

## Structure Rules (Hard Limits)

### Email Length
- **Maximum 80 words per email** (NEVER exceed)
- See: [[email-length-80-words-maximum]]

### Sentence Length
- **Maximum 30 words per sentence** (ideally 25 words)
- Space out sentences - avoid chunky blocks

### Reading Level
- **Grade 9 reading comprehension level**
- Readable and understandable, but not dumbed down like talking to children

### Formatting Prohibitions
- ❌ **NO bullet points** - flesh out ideas in sentences
- ❌ **NO em-dashes or en-dashes** (— or –)
- ❌ **Maximum ONE hyphen** (-) per email
- ❌ **NO uncommon symbols** (→, ←, •, *, etc.)
- ❌ **NO dollar signs** - write "usd" or "15k" or "15M" instead of "$15"

## Campaign Structure (All Clients)

### 2-Step Maximum
- Email 1: **3 variants (A/B/C)**
- Email 2: **2 variants (A/B)**

### Campaign Name Format
```
[version] | [client name] | [core theme ≤5 words] | [account ICP] | [target persona] | [copy format] | [launch date]
```

**Example:**
```
v2 | Spotlight Social | growth math & profitable scaling | DTC stores $5M+ revenue | founder and VP marketing | front end offer | Jan 5th
```

## Variant Testing Rules

### Subject Line Testing
- ❌ **NEVER test subject lines**
- ✅ All variants use **SAME subject line**

### Copy Testing
- ✅ Test copy elements only:
  - Hook styles (data vs story vs question)
  - CTA types (soft vs direct vs binary)
  - Copy structure (touch/see/feel vs logical)
  - Offer framing (problem-first vs solution-first)
  - Social proof placement (integrated vs omitted)

### Meaningful Differences Only
- ✅ Variants must test **meaningful lifts**
- ❌ NOT trivial changes:
  - "was" → "were"
  - Reordering same sentences
  - Synonyms without strategic intent
  - Minor word swaps

**Example of meaningful test:**
- Variant A: Data-driven stat hook ("87% of DTC brands see creative fatigue in 3 weeks")
- Variant B: Story-driven insight hook ("Saw you're scaling to $5M - that's when creative fatigue hits hardest")

## Content Requirements

### Touch/See/Feel (Sensory Language)
- **At least one variant** must use touch/see/feel words
- See: [[see-feel-touch-words]]
- Makes abstract concepts concrete and memorable

### CTAs (Call-to-Action)
- **Sell the promise land** of the offer
- No pressure, non-salesy
- Deeply clear and straightforward
- Tied to a goal inferred from copy

**Good CTAs:**
- "Open to a quick intro next week to see if an adoption clarity pass could help with some dropoffs you're seeing?"
- "Would you be open to a quick intro sometime this week or next to see if it makes sense for {{company}}?"

**Bad CTAs:**
- "Want to chat?" (doesn't sell transformation)
- "Let me know" (no clear ask)
- "Book a demo" (too salesy/pushy)

### Social Proof
- Integrate naturally (NOT jammed in)
- Can save for Email 2/3 if it bloats Email 1
- Reword case studies to fit context
- Blend seamlessly into email flow

### Persona/Context Awareness
- Root offer/copy in prospect's **day-to-day situation**
- Don't use internal descriptors literally in copy
  - Example: If ICP is "brands doing $50k-$150k monthly revenue," don't say "hey, if you're making between $50k to $150k monthly..."
  - Prospects don't identify with your internal labels
- Research how they refer to themselves collectively

### Email Structure and Flow
- **Get into the email immediately** - no soft openings ever
  - ❌ BAD: "Quick thought...", "Just wanted to...", "Hope this finds you well..."
  - ✅ GOOD: Jump straight into value, question, or statement
- **No meta-commentary about following up**
  - ❌ BAD: "Following up on...", "Circling back...", "Touching base about..."
  - ✅ GOOD: Just continue the conversation naturally
  - Why: Wastes words, sounds robotic, states the obvious
- **Natural conversational flow**
  - ❌ BAD: Choppy sentences that read like bullet points ("Free blueprint. Shows what's manual. Delivered in 14 days.")
  - ✅ GOOD: Flowing sentences that sound like natural speech
  - Test: Read aloud - if it sounds like presentation slides, rewrite

### Context Clarity (CRITICAL for High-Stakes Offers)

When the offer involves **high-stakes actions** (buying/selling businesses, partnerships, investments, major commitments), context clarity is MANDATORY.

**High-stakes indicators:**
- Acquisition/exit conversations
- Business sales (buying or selling)
- Partnership proposals
- Investment opportunities
- Major service commitments

**Requirements for high-stakes emails:**

1. **Opening line clarifies primary action/intent**
   - ❌ BAD: "Most craft businesses spend 6 months finding buyers"
   - ✅ GOOD: "We buy pottery businesses and even closed a similar business in 8 weeks last year"
   - Why: Recipient immediately knows this is about acquiring their business

2. **Dual paths/options are explicit and concrete**
   - ❌ BAD: "We offer two paths: traditional market process or fast close"
   - ✅ GOOD: "We introduce you to buyers in our network, or we buy {{Company}} directly"
   - Why: No vague abstractions - recipient knows exactly what each path means

3. **CTAs include qualifying language**
   - ❌ BAD: "Worth a quick call to discuss?"
   - ✅ GOOD: "If you're looking to sell, worth a quick call to see which fits?"
   - Why: Self-selection reduces wasted time, clarifies conversation scope

4. **Concrete deliverables, not abstractions**
   - ❌ BAD: "We provide comprehensive market process optimization"
   - ✅ GOOD: "30-min exploratory call to discuss two exit options"
   - Why: Recipient knows exactly what they're signing up for

**When to apply:**
- Always for acquisition/exit outreach
- Always when asking prospect to make major business decision
- Always when context confusion = significant wasted time for both parties

**Examples:**

**Good (Clear Context):**
```
Hi {{FirstName}},

We buy pottery businesses and even closed a similar business in 8 weeks last year.

You'd have two options: we introduce you to buyers in our network, or we buy {{Company}} directly.

If you're looking to sell, worth a quick call to see which fits?
```

**Bad (Vague Context):**
```
Hi {{FirstName}},

Most craft businesses take 6+ months to exit. We specialize in streamlining market processes.

Want to discuss your options?
```

## Three Email Formats

### 1. Front-End Offer
Pushes a lead magnet or low-friction offer

**Structure:**
- Hook/context
- Problem statement
- Solution/offer presentation
- CTA

**Example:**
```
Hey {{FirstName}},

We've seen big usage drop-offs (and a spike in support tickets) across digital care apps when key screens don't click fast.

If you're seeing this, we can help with a "Workflow Clarity Pass": we review 3–5 core screens, deliver a short pattern pack (what's unclear, why it matters, edge cases), and include an updated UI in Figma

Usually takes 7–10 days. We did this for Viome across web + mobile (and implemented improvements for them in under two months).

Interested?
```

### 2. Poke the Bear
Gauge interest/pain internally, soft CTA

**Structure:**
- Surface BIGGEST problem
- How you solve it / how you've solved it for others + promise land
- Soft CTA

**Example:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

When you onboard a new client with different data format needs, does it add to the integration backlog on IT's plate?

If every shipper you bring on turns into a small project (custom mappings, one-off rules, and fixes that break later), Logmozo can make onboarding repeatable (with an AI layer that makes sense of different data formats from shippers).

Is simplifying onboarding a focus in Q1?
```

### 3. One-Liner
Ultra-brief, 32 words max (excluding signature)

**Structure:**
- USP/MVO + mini case study in 1-2 sentences
- NEVER more than 32 words

**Example:**
```
{{first_name}}, interested in a no-cost profit sheet showing your max CAC, min MER and two levers to scale without sacrificing margin? (same unlock that helped us scale Freshly Picked from 5-15M)
```

## Quality Checklist (Pre-Flight)

Before any campaign is presented to user:

### Hard Rules
- [ ] All emails ≤80 words
- [ ] All sentences ≤30 words (ideally ≤25)
- [ ] Grade 9 reading level
- [ ] No bullet points
- [ ] No em/en dashes, max 1 hyphen
- [ ] No dollar signs or uncommon symbols
- [ ] All variants use SAME subject line
- [ ] Campaign name follows format

### Content Quality
- [ ] At least one variant uses touch/see/feel
- [ ] CTAs sell promise land (not pushy)
- [ ] Social proof integrated naturally (if included)
- [ ] Rooted in prospect's day-to-day context
- [ ] Variants test meaningful differences (not trivial)
- [ ] Natural conversational flow (not choppy bullet-point sentences)
- [ ] NO meta-commentary about following up (just follow up)
- [ ] NO soft openings (get into email immediately)

### Context Clarity (for High-Stakes Offers)
- [ ] Opening line clarifies primary action/intent (if high-stakes)
- [ ] Dual paths/options are explicit, not vague (if applicable)
- [ ] CTAs include qualifying language (if high-stakes)
- [ ] Concrete deliverables, not abstractions (always)

### Knowledge Alignment
- [ ] Uses validated patterns from client KB
- [ ] Avoids anti-patterns from client KB
- [ ] Links to ≥3 knowledge nodes
- [ ] Follows format-specific structure (front-end offer / poke the bear / one-liner)

# How to Invalidate Cold Email as a Channel

Three systematic tests to definitively determine if cold email isn't viable for your business. If all three fail, cold email may not be your channel - pivot to different customer acquisition strategy.

## Core Principles

- Most founders keep trying without systematic invalidation
- Better to know channel doesn't work than waste months
- Three escalating tests: feedback → free value → paid meetings
- If you can't pay people to be interested, fundamental problem exists
- Channel fit matters more than perfect execution

## When to Use

Run these tests when:
- Multiple campaigns have failed
- Months of effort with no traction
- Questioning if cold email is right channel
- Need definitive answer before pivoting
- Stakeholders want proof of channel viability

## How to Apply

### TEST 1: Pure Feedback Ask (Remove Sales Pressure)

**Remove meeting request entirely. Just ask for feedback.**

**Template:**
```
Subject: feedback?

Hey {{first_name}},

I'm reaching out purely for feedback about [product/service].

What are you currently doing to solve [problem]?
What would it take to get you to consider another solution?

Very upfront - NOT trying to book a meeting with you.

So far we've been failing on messaging and need your help.
```

**What You're Testing:**
- Will anyone engage if no sales pressure?
- Do they even have the problem you're solving?
- What objections surface?

**Success Signal:**
- Getting honest feedback about the problem
- Learning their current solution
- Understanding real objections

**Failure Signal:**
- No responses even with zero sales pressure
- Suggests: wrong ICP, or problem doesn't exist/matter

---

### TEST 2: Escalating Free Offer (Find Value Threshold)

**Slowly increase what you'll give away for free.**

**Process:**

**Level 1:** Standard offer
"We provide [service]"
→ No responses

**Level 2:** Free trial/demo
"Try it free for 30 days"
→ No responses

**Level 3:** Free partial service
"We'll do [specific deliverable] for free"
→ No responses

**Level 4:** Significant free work
"We'll do complete [project component] for free"
→ No responses

**Level 5:** Almost full service free
"We'll deliver [major value] at no cost"
→ If STILL no responses...

**What You're Testing:**
- At what point does offer become irresistible?
- Is there ANY level of free value that generates interest?
- Can this be a repeatable process or too expensive?

**Success Signal:**
- Find threshold where responses come in
- Can turn this into sustainable front-end offer
- Validates that value exists, just pricing/positioning issue

**Failure Signal:**
- Even extreme free offers get ignored
- Suggests: wrong market, or outcome isn't valuable
- Cost to acquire would never be profitable

**Key Question:**
"If you can't get responses even giving away the farm, do you have product-market fit?"

---

### TEST 3: Paid Meeting Test (The Definitive Test)

**Offer to PAY prospects $50-100 for a meeting.**

**Template:**
```
Subject: $100 for 30 minutes

Hey {{first_name}},

[Plainly state your offer/service]

I'd like to pay you $100 for a 30-minute meeting to discuss if this would be valuable for [company].

No obligation, just want to understand your perspective.

Interested?
```

**What You're Testing:**
- Will anyone engage even if PAID to do so?
- $50-100 isn't enough to make someone waste 30 min (if wrong ICP)
- But is enough to get attention if remotely relevant

**Success Signal:**
- 1%+ positive response rate
- People take money AND engage in real discussion
- Validates problem exists, you're reaching right people

**Failure Signal:**
- <1% positive response rate even with payment
- People won't take free money for 30 minutes
- **Definitive proof:** Either wrong targeting or offer has no market

**Why This Works:**

Contrary to belief, most people won't waste 30 minutes for $100 unless:
1. They're right ICP (have the problem)
2. Offer is remotely relevant
3. They're at least curious

**If they won't take your money:**
- Dramatically rethink targeting
- Dramatically rethink offer
- OR pivot to different channel/customer journey

## Decision Tree

```
Run Test 1 (Feedback Ask):
└─ Responses + useful feedback?
   ├─ YES → Problem exists, messaging issue. Iterate.
   └─ NO → Run Test 2

Run Test 2 (Escalating Free Offer):
└─ Responses at some free offer level?
   ├─ YES → Problem exists, positioning issue. Build front-end offer.
   └─ NO → Run Test 3

Run Test 3 (Paid Meetings):
└─ 1%+ positive response rate?
   ├─ YES → Channel viable, offer/messaging needs work
   └─ NO → Cold email is NOT your channel
              Consider: Inbound, partnerships, events, different ICP
```

## Example Good Email

```
Subject: 15 hours/week?

Hi [Name],

Most sales teams we work with waste ~15 hours per week
reconciling data between their CRM and product analytics.

We built [Product] to eliminate that. One unified view,
real-time sync, no manual work.

Worth a 15-min chat?

[Signature]
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific pain point (15 hours/week)
- Clear solution
- Low-friction CTA
- No fluff

---

## Example Bad Email

```
Subject: Transform Your Sales Operations!

Dear [Name],

I hope this email finds you well! We're revolutionizing
the sales technology landscape with our cutting-edge,
AI-powered, best-in-class solution...

[3 more paragraphs of marketing speak]
```

**Why this fails:**
- Generic subject line
- Corporate jargon
- No specific pain point
- Too long

---

## Approved Phrases/Language

**For pain points:**
- "waste X hours per week"
- "losing deals because..."
- "manually reconciling..."

**For solution:**
- "We built [X] to [specific outcome]"
- "eliminates [pain point]"
- "X minutes to [result]"

**For CTA:**
- "Worth a 15-min chat?"
- "Quick call to show you?"
- "Want to see how?"
